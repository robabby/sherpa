## 🧭 Strategic Framing for Rob's Job Search

The best content *right now* is content that:

1. **Shows the work**, not just talks about it — HBR's article says AI has devalued generic thought leadership. Specificity wins.
2. **Names the numbers** — 472 PRs, 11 weeks, 6 apps, 2 months solo. These are attention-grabbing proof points that no competitor has.
3. **Bridges the credibility gap** — companies like Linear, Airbnb, Vercel aren't just hiring for AI skills; they're hiring for *judgment*. Content that demonstrates how you think under constraint (shipping fast, staying opinionated, knowing what to not build) is more valuable than pure tutorial content.
4. **Occupies the authentic practitioner lane** — there are a thousand "here's how to use Claude" posts. There's almost nobody writing from the perspective of someone who built an AI consulting practice *and* production products *and* is targeting specific companies.

**Recommended first post to publish:** #1 (the 472 PRs story). Fastest path to a viral HN moment, directly tied to Rob's unique proof points, and creates a content foundation to reference in every subsequent piece.
