## Platform Analysis

### LinkedIn: "Open to Work" vs. Thought Leadership for Senior Engineers

This is a real strategic decision with data behind it.

**The case FOR "Open to Work" (Recruiters Only mode):**
- Increases recruiter InMail response rates by ~40% (Interview Guys 2026)
- 65% of successful hires in 2026 initiated through LinkedIn profile conversion (Jobright data)
- "Open to Work" (private to recruiters) works like a passive signal amplifier — gets you into more recruiter searches
- Adding GitHub link boosts tech recruiter contact rates by ~23% (ResuFit analysis of 42 users)

**The case AGAINST the public green banner for Rob specifically:**
- The public banner can create perception of desperation, especially at senior/staff levels
- "Senior candidates benefit from stronger alternative signals (referrals, thought leadership, quantified outcomes) so that Open to Work is secondary—not defining" — Careery research
- The banner invites spray-and-pray recruiter outreach for roles that aren't relevant — low signal/noise
- In status-sensitive contexts (high-caliber tech companies, staff/principal level), hiring managers form negative impressions before reading the profile
- The key data point: "For senior executive roles, the calculus changes and a more targeted, private approach often performs better" — Interview Guys 2026

**The winning formula for Rob (non-employed, senior engineer):**
1. Enable **"Recruiters Only"** visibility on Open to Work (not the public banner)
2. Add GitHub link to profile (23% boost in tech recruiter contacts)
3. Optimize headline with format: `Role | Value | Differentiators` → e.g., "Staff Frontend Engineer | Agentic Development | React/TypeScript/Swift"
4. Post **thought leadership content consistently** — this builds the "topic DNA" that LinkedIn's 360Brew algo uses for organic distribution
5. The thought leadership + private signal combo is demonstrably more effective than the green banner for senior engineers

**Key data point to internalize:** GitHub links boost tech recruiter contacts 23% — that's basically free. Rob should have robabby.com and his GitHub prominently linked right now if he doesn't.

### LinkedIn Native Video: Underutilized by Engineers, Algorithm-Favored

Native video is **the second highest-performing format on LinkedIn** (after document carousels), but significantly underused by developers:

- Native video gets ~4-5% engagement rate (vs ~2% text, ~6.6% carousels)
- LinkedIn **autoplays native video** in the feed — passive viewership before any click
- Video is suppressed when posted as an external YouTube link (same external link penalty as blog links)
- **Must be uploaded directly to LinkedIn** to get the algorithm boost

**What performs well (tech engineer video on LinkedIn):**
- "Talking head" videos: developer/engineer explaining a real challenge or decision they made — 60-90 seconds
- Value-first hooks: first 3 seconds must stop the scroll; lead with the problem or the number
- Text overlays + captions: majority of viewers watch without sound — critical for reach
- Behind-the-scenes: your workflow, your setup, a moment where something broke

**For Rob:** A 60-second "here's how I structure a Claude Code session for a non-trivial feature" would likely perform significantly. Low production overhead — phone + screen recording + captions. Repost workflow: record once, caption it for LinkedIn native, also upload to YouTube for SEO.

### YouTube: Long-term Authority Engine

YouTube is **search engine first, social platform second** — this is the key difference. Content posted today can surface in search results for years.

**What works for engineering channels:**
- Tutorial series with clear chapters and searchable titles
- Problem-framing videos ("I hit this bug and here's how I debugged it")
- "How I built X" walkthroughs — structured narrative, not just code dump
- Consistent naming patterns: "Agentic Engineering with Claude Code: [Episode #]"

YouTube is not a fast ROI channel — months to build. But it's where someone like Simon Willison builds long-term authority. **For Rob's timeline (active job hunt now), YouTube is secondary to LinkedIn.** But starting a "working in public" series that lives on YouTube and clips to LinkedIn is a reasonable long-game play if he has bandwidth.
