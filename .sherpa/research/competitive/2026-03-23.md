## Summary

The public conversation around AI-accelerated development is hitting an inflection point in early 2026. The year opened with two competing narratives:

1. **The promise:** Agentic engineering enables experienced engineers to operate at dramatically higher velocity by orchestrating AI agents rather than writing code line-by-line. The "solo founder + AI = small team" archetype is now proven and celebrated.
2. **The reckoning:** "Vibe coding" (Karpathy's Feb 2025 term) produced a hangover — security issues, comprehension debt, and a wave of low-quality AI PRs flooding open source. The discourse is correcting toward more structured, spec-first, review-intensive workflows.

For Rob specifically: the positioning sweet spot is **experienced engineer who uses AI structurally, not recklessly** — the person who bridges the gap between "vibe coder" and "agentic engineer." That's a credible, underrepresented voice.

## Terminology & Framing in Use

### The Main Terms (ranked by currency)

| Term | Origin | Meaning | Status |
|------|---------|---------|--------|
| **Vibe coding** | Karpathy, Feb 2025 | Reckless prompting, accepting AI output without review | Passé / cautionary |
| **Agentic engineering** | Karpathy, Feb 2026 | Orchestrating agents while acting as architect/reviewer | Current hot term |
| **AI-assisted programming** | Simon Willison | LLM as typing assistant; human still understands all code | Steady/academic |
| **AI-accelerated development** | Industry | Broad term for using AI to ship faster | Generic/safe |
| **AI-native** | Various | Built with AI from the ground up; AI-first architecture | Platform/product framing |
| **Agentic workflows** | Andrew Ng, McKinsey | Multi-step, iterative AI task execution | Enterprise/academic |
| **Solo founder + AI** | Pieter Levels, IndieHackers | One person building what used to require a team | Entrepreneurial framing |
| **Founding engineer** | Job market | Engineer #1 at an AI-native startup | Hiring trend term |

### Framing Patterns to Watch
- **Architect/conductor/orchestrator** — the new metaphor for what experienced engineers do with AI
- **"Specify, delegate, validate"** — emerging workflow mantra
- **"The agentic engineer's job is to specify problems, select and configure agents, validate their outputs"** (Sesame Disk Group)
- **"Factory model"** — Addy Osmani's framing of multi-agent pipelines as a software factory
- **"Human on the loop"** vs "human in the loop" — distinction emerging between oversight models
- **"Comprehension debt"** — low-quality code that works but nobody understands (GitHub/trick77)
- **"Haunted codebase"** — AI-generated code that developers are afraid to touch

## Current Discourse & Debates

### Debate 1: Does AI Actually Increase Productivity?
**The paradox:** 93% developer AI adoption, but only ~10% company-level productivity gains (Faros AI, 2025)
- METR study (July 2025): Experienced open-source developers were **not measurably sped up** by early 2025 AI tools — and *overestimated* their speedup by ~20%
- METR update (Feb 2026): Believes developers are more sped up now in early 2026 than the July 2025 estimates
- Gergely Orosz: "Speed of typing was never the bottleneck"
- AI-authored code: now **26.9% of all production code** (up from 22% last quarter, Feb 2026 data)

**Synthesis:** Individual velocity gains are real; organizational productivity gains lag. The skill is knowing *which* bottlenecks AI actually removes.

### Debate 2: Vibe Coding → Backlash → Agentic Engineering Correction
- 2025: Vibe coding went mainstream. Demos looked great. Production deployments didn't.
- Open source maintainers fought back: Daniel Stenberg shut down cURL's bug bounty after 20% of submissions were AI. Mitchell Hashimoto banned AI code from Ghostty. Steve Ruiz closed all external PRs to tldraw.
- Research paper: "Vibe Coding Kills Open Source"
- 2026 response: Agentic engineering discourse emphasizes review, understanding, spec-first
- Hacker News commenter (March 2026): "After three months of seeing what agentic engineering produces first-hand, I think there's going to be a pretty big correction."

### Debate 3: Junior Dev Apocalypse vs. Senior Dev Leverage
- Yahoo Finance/Karpathy story: "Growing demand for experienced engineers who can operate in the agentic paradigm, and sharply reduced demand for entry-level roles"
- The pipeline problem: Junior roles were the training ground for senior engineers. If those disappear, who becomes the next senior?
- Counter-narrative: Some argue AI raises the floor for all developers, democratizing access

### Debate 4: Enterprise Readiness
- IBM: 2026 should be "the year multi-agent systems move into production"
- But: Only 9% of enterprises have reached "Ready" AI governance maturity (Deloitte)
- McKinsey: Employees without technical backgrounds can learn to manage agentic workflows as quickly as trained engineers
- The "orchestration layer" is the new critical skill — how agents hand off work and resolve conflicts

## Implications for Rob's Positioning & Voice

### 1. The Naming Gap Rob Can Own
The term "agentic engineering" is Karpathy's and already has Google/Addy Osmani behind it. But there's a **practitioner voice gap** — most prominent writers are researchers, academics, or journalists. Rob would be a **Staff Engineer who did the thing** and can speak from inside the work.

**Differentiated angle:** "I didn't write about agentic engineering. I lived it for 11 weeks. Here's what the theory misses."

### 2. The Credibility Stack
Rob's story maps well to the things people actually care about:
- **Staff-level experience** = exactly the profile that benefits most from AI leverage (orchestrator role, not just code monkey)
- **11 weeks of intensive AI-first building** = experiential authority, not pundit authority
- **Working in the open** with real projects (WavePoint, Sherpa) = can show, not just tell
- **The "solo founder + AI" thesis** is proven by Pieter Levels and others — Rob is living a version of this

### 3. The Tone Gap
The current landscape splits into:
- **Breathless evangelism** (solo founder bros, LinkedIn hustle culture)
- **Dry academic skepticism** (METR, Pragmatic Engineer)
- **Enterprise jargon** (McKinsey, IBM)

**Rob's tone** (warm, opinionated, practical) would be refreshing. There's almost no one writing about this from the perspective of: *"I'm an experienced engineer who lost my job, used this hard time to go all-in on AI-accelerated development, and here's what I learned that nobody else is saying."*

### 4. Terminology Recommendations
- Use **"agentic engineering"** — it's the current term, has credibility (Karpathy/Osmani), and Rob's story fits it
- Avoid **"vibe coding"** — it's passé and carries a reckless connotation
- Consider **"AI-accelerated development"** as a softer, more legible term for non-technical hiring audiences
- The metaphors that resonate: **orchestrator, architect, conductor** — not "prompt engineer" (low status)
- Rob can credibly claim: "Staff engineer operating as an agentic engineer" — that combination is distinctive

### 5. Content Angle Opportunities
- **The productivity paradox from the inside:** Gergely Orosz critiques "AI hasn't moved the needle" — Rob can nuance this with real experience of where it does and doesn't work
- **What 11 weeks of intensive AI-first dev actually teaches you** — nobody has written this from a credible senior eng perspective
- **The "haunted codebase" problem** — a specific, real issue Rob can speak to with authority from his WavePoint/Sherpa work
- **Building at team scale solo** — Rob IS living this; connect to the Pieter Levels / solo founder archetype but from an engineering craft angle

### 6. The Job Market Angle
The bifurcated job market finding is key for Rob's consulting pitch: experienced engineers who can operate in the agentic paradigm are increasingly valuable, not less. That's the counter-narrative to "AI is taking engineer jobs" — and it's backed by data.
