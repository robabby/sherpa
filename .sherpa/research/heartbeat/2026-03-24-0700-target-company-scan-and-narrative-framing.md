## Task 2: Narrative Framing Research

### How to Present Solo / Personal Projects at Staff Level

#### The Core Tension
Rob built two production products (Sherpa, WavePoint) that could be framed as:
1. **Founded companies** → signals entrepreneur, not employee mindset; raises "will they leave?" concerns
2. **Side projects** → undersells the scale (472+ PRs in 11 weeks, 6 native apps, full platforms)
3. **Independent engineering work / consulting** → frames as craft, not company-building

**The consensus: frame as Independent Engineering Practice, not founding.**

---

#### Recommended Framing Patterns

**Pattern 1: "Independent Work" as a Work Experience Entry**
```
Sherpa Studio / Sherpa Solar — Independent Engineering  |  Jan 2025–Present
AI transformation consulting practice + agentic engineering workflow suite (Sherpa Studio).
• Architected and shipped full-stack Next.js platform + agentic orchestration tooling (MCP servers, AI workflow automation)
• 472+ PRs shipped solo in 11 weeks; 6 production Swift apps (WavePoint iOS/macOS)
• Built to demonstrate AI-native engineering capacity: designed, shipped, and operated production systems end-to-end
```
**Why this works:** Lists it in the experience section (not a "projects" sidebar), uses engineering verbs not founder verbs, quantifies output aggressively.

**Pattern 2: "Consulting Practice + Product Build" Framing**
Per resumeworded.com: "It's fine to create [a consulting firm name] if work was project-based."
```
Sherpa Consulting — Principal Engineer & Founder  |  2025–Present
Built AI-native engineering consulting practice while developing two production products as proof of capability:
...
```
**Why this sometimes works:** Honest, transparent, signals intentionality. Risk: "founder" label may prime "startup brain" perception.

**Pattern 3: Separate "Selected Projects" Section**
For someone with Rob's seniority, the Quora consensus on this format:
```
SELECTED PROJECTS
Sherpa Studio | Creator / Solo Engineer | Next.js, MCP, TypeScript, Swift
  → Full agentic workflow suite for AI-native development. 472 PRs in 11 weeks (solo).
WavePoint | Creator / Solo Engineer | Swift, Next.js, MCP
  → Astrology platform: web + 6 native Apple apps + MCP server. Live in App Store.
```
**Why this works for Rob specifically:** Keeps it honest (doesn't inflate to "founded a company"), lets the numbers do the work, easy to contextualize in a cover note.

---

#### The "Founded" vs "Built" Question

**Key insight from career community consensus:**
- "Founded" = signal of entrepreneurial intent → companies worry you'll leave or want more autonomy than they can give
- "Built" or "Created" = engineering signal → shows craft, initiative, and capability without the startup baggage
- If the project had actual clients/revenue, "Consulting" or "Independent Practice" is the right frame — it's accurate AND engineer-coded
- If it was built primarily as demonstration of capability: be honest in the narrative. It's fine to say "I built this to develop and demonstrate AI-native engineering capacity" — especially for companies like Augment Code that explicitly value product taste and self-direction.

**From Augment Code's hiring blog (2 weeks ago):**
> "The engineers who create the most leverage are the ones with product taste, architectural judgment, and the ability to direct systems of humans and agents toward the right outcome."
This is the exact narrative Rob should lead with. Building Sherpa and WavePoint *is the proof* of those dimensions.

---

#### Examples / Patterns That Work

1. **"Sabbatical + Project" Frame** (from SE Stack Exchange community)
   - List as separate resume section: "Independent Engineering" or "Sabbatical Project"
   - Show 3 things: what you built, what you learned, what you shipped
   - Works well for gap coverage AND demonstrates agency

2. **"Product Engineering Lead" title for solo work** (Quora community)
   - Using "Founder / Product & Engineering Lead" on solo projects is acceptable and common
   - Metric-heavy bullets shift the narrative from "role" to "impact"
   - Example seen in wild: "Built and launched SaaS for X; acquired 1,200 paying users..."
   - Rob's version: "Shipped 472 PRs solo in 11 weeks; 6 live App Store apps; full agentic workflow platform in production"

3. **Lead with the throughline, not the company name**
   - Instead of: "Sherpa Solar — Founder"
   - Use: "AI-Native Engineering (Independent) — architecting and shipping production AI systems solo"
   - The company name is secondary; the work is primary

---

#### What Rob Should NOT Do

- Don't downplay the projects as "side projects" — they're too substantial
- Don't lead with "Founder" or "CEO" for both — creates employer anxiety about commitment
- Don't hide them — they're the strongest proof points in his pitch
- Don't skip the numbers — 472 PRs, 11 weeks, 6 apps, production deployment = the whole argument

---

#### Suggested Cover Letter Throughline

> "In 2025, rather than take another staff role immediately, I built two production AI systems from scratch — a consulting practice with an agentic engineering workflow suite (Sherpa Studio), and a full-stack astrology platform with 6 native Apple apps (WavePoint). I did this because I wanted to prove — to myself and to future employers — that I could operate as an AI-native engineer: designing, building, shipping, and operating production systems end-to-end without a team. The output speaks for itself: 472 PRs in 11 weeks, all solo. I'm ready to bring that capacity and judgment to [Company]."

This framing:
- Acknowledges the gap proactively
- Reframes it as intentional, not accidental
- Leads with the numbers
- Positions as "engineer who can do this" not "entrepreneur who wants autonomy"
