## Why This Matters

The `<title>` tag appears in:
- Google search results (the blue clickable link)
- Browser tabs
- Social media previews (LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Slack link unfurls)
- Bookmark names

When a recruiter or hiring manager shares Rob's site link in Slack or clicks through from LinkedIn, "Rob Abby - Staff Frontend Engineer" is what they see. That's not who Rob is right now.

## Title Tag Best Practices (2026)

- **50-60 characters** — Google truncates at ~60. Primary keyword near the front.
- **Format:** `[Name] — [Role/Positioning] | [Differentiator]`
- **Include searchable terms** recruiters would use
- **No keyword stuffing** — must read naturally

## Recommended Title Options

### Option 1 (Recommended)
```
Rob Abby — Founder & Engineer | AI-Native Development
```
54 characters. Leads with name, establishes founder identity, includes "AI-Native" keyword.

### Option 2 (ATS-optimized)
```
Rob Abby — Staff Engineer & Founder | Sherpa · WavePoint
```
57 characters. Includes both "Staff Engineer" for recruiter search and founder identity.

### Option 3 (Narrative-forward)
```
Rob Abby — Building at Team Scale with AI | Sherpa
```
51 characters. More conversational, differentiating.

### Option 4 (Minimal)
```
Rob Abby — Engineer & Founder
```
30 characters. Clean, lets the content speak.

**Recommendation:** Option 2. It preserves "Staff Engineer" for ATS/recruiter discoverability while adding the founder identity and company names. Both Sherpa and WavePoint become searchable brand terms.

## Meta Description

Should be 150-160 characters. Must include: who Rob is, what he built, that he's available.

**Recommended:**
```
Founder & Principal Engineer at Sherpa and WavePoint. 472+ PRs in 11 weeks, 6 native apps, AI dev tooling. 13 years of frontend infrastructure at scale.
```
155 characters. Hits all the proof points, includes numbers for credibility.

**Alternative:**
```
Staff engineer turned founder. Built a consulting practice, a full-stack product, and the AI dev tooling that powered it. Now seeking a team to join.
```
151 characters. More narrative, includes the "seeking a team" signal.
