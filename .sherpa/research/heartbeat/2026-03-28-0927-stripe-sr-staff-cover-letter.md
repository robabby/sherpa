## Cover Letter

The Merchant Experience team owns the surface where every Stripe product becomes real to the people who use it. That's a frontend architecture problem at a scale most engineers never encounter — and it's exactly the kind of problem I've spent my career learning to solve.

Over the past year, I built two production products solo: Sherpa Studio, an agentic engineering workflow suite, and WavePoint, an astrology platform with a Next.js web app and six native Apple apps. 472 pull requests merged in 11 weeks. Both are data-dense, cross-cutting applications — the kind where component architecture, design system discipline, and thoughtful state management aren't nice-to-haves but prerequisites for shipping at all. The Dashboard's challenge of unifying payments, billing, connect, and disputes under a coherent merchant-facing experience is a version of the same architectural problem I've been solving across my own product surfaces.

What draws me to this role specifically is the intersection of frontend craft and organizational influence. Senior Staff means setting technical direction across teams, not just within one — and that's where I've done my best work. In 13+ years as a frontend engineer, the pattern has been consistent: I identify the architectural decisions that compound across the organization, build consensus around them, and then ship the proof. At my last role as Staff Frontend Engineer, that meant leading design system adoption and cross-team frontend platform work. With Sherpa and WavePoint, it meant designing component systems and monorepo architectures that let a single engineer maintain the velocity of a team.

I'm particularly interested in Stripe's AI-powered design system migration work. I've been building AI-native engineering tooling with Sherpa Studio — not just using AI assistants, but architecting the workflows and infrastructure that make AI-assisted development reliable and reproducible. That experience gives me a perspective on design system evolution and large-scale codebase migrations that's both practical and forward-looking.

The presentation round in Stripe's interview process sounds like exactly the conversation I want to have. I have two shipped products with real architecture stories — decisions about component boundaries, state management at scale, and the tradeoffs of building fast without accumulating debt. I'd welcome the chance to walk through them.

Rob Abby
