## How LinkedIn's Algorithm Works in 2026

LinkedIn now runs a **3-layer ranking system** that determines which profiles recruiters see:

1. **Boolean Matching** — Do your keywords match the recruiter's search terms? If not, you're invisible. This scans headline, title, skills, experience descriptions.
2. **Profile Strength Score** — How complete, endorsed, and active is your profile? Determines rank position among keyword matches.
3. **Relevance & Proximity** — Mutual connections, network proximity, engagement signals. The final tiebreaker.

LinkedIn's new **Hiring Assistant** (AI agent for recruiters) runs automated searches and ranks candidates. Early adopters report reviewing 81% fewer profiles to find matches and 66% higher InMail acceptance rates.

## Specific Actions for Rob (Priority Order)

### 1. Headline — Use All 220 Characters

The headline is the **highest-weight field** in LinkedIn search. It's the first thing Boolean search scans and appears in every search result snippet.

**Formula:** `[Target Role] | [Specialty] | [2-4 Core Skills] | [Value Statement]`

**Recommended for Rob:**
```
Founder & Principal Engineer | Sherpa · WavePoint | React · TypeScript · Next.js · AI-Native Development | 472+ PRs shipped solo in 11 weeks
```
(218 characters — nearly maxed)

**Why this works:**
- "Staff Frontend Engineer" and "Principal Engineer" are both searchable titles recruiters use
- "React · TypeScript · Next.js" are the exact Boolean terms recruiters type
- "AI-Native Development" matches Meta's literal job title keyword
- "472+ PRs shipped solo in 11 weeks" is the attention-grabber that gets clicks

**Alternative (if prioritizing ATS/recruiter search):**
```
Staff Frontend Engineer · Founder | React · TypeScript · Next.js · Swift | AI-Native Development · Agentic Engineering | Open to Staff+ Roles
```

### 2. Skills Section — Add 50+ Skills

LinkedIn now allows **100 skills**, and this section is **directly searchable** in LinkedIn Recruiter. It's one of 30+ filters recruiters use.

**Must-add skills for Rob:**
- **Core:** React, TypeScript, Next.js, JavaScript, Node.js, HTML5, CSS3, Swift, SwiftUI
- **Frameworks/Tools:** Tailwind CSS, Radix UI, GraphQL, REST APIs, Storybook, Vite, pnpm
- **AI/Agentic:** Claude Code, AI-Augmented Development, Agentic Engineering, MCP, Prompt Engineering, AI Workflow Design
- **Infrastructure:** AWS, Docker, CI/CD, Vercel, Supabase, Stripe API
- **Leadership:** Design Systems, Technical Architecture, Mentorship, Cross-Functional Collaboration, Code Review
- **Methodology:** Agile, Lean UX, User Research, Accessibility (WCAG), Performance Optimization

**Pin top 3:** React, TypeScript, AI-Augmented Development

### 3. Open to Work — Recruiter-Only

For Rob's level (Staff/Principal), **use recruiter-only, NOT the public green banner.**

The data:
- "Open to Work" members are 40% more likely to receive recruiter InMails
- BUT: high-level headhunters report the public green banner signals desperation (Reddit thread with 1.1K upvotes confirms this perception)
- Recruiter-only setting gets the discovery benefit without the stigma
- Rob is a founder currently running two companies — the public "looking for work" banner would undercut the narrative

**Set preferences to:**
- Recruiter-only visibility
- Target titles: Staff Frontend Engineer, Staff Software Engineer, Principal Engineer, AI Product Engineer
- Locations: Remote, Seattle WA, Bellingham WA
- Job types: Full-time

### 4. About Section — Hook in First 2 Lines

The first 2 lines show before the "See more" button. They must grab attention immediately.

**Use the draft from the copy research:**
> I build products at the intersection of systems engineering and AI-augmented development. Over the past year, I founded two companies and shipped three products — solo, at a pace that used to require a team.

Then expand into the full narrative (already drafted in heartbeat research `2026-03-21-1820-sherpa-copy-drafts.md`).

### 5. Experience — P.A.R. Format

Use **Problem → Action → Result** for each bullet:
- ❌ "Responsible for frontend development"
- ✅ "Shipped 472+ PRs across a Next.js web app and 6 native Apple apps in 11 weeks by designing a behavioral governance framework for AI agent workflows"

Specific tools and technologies in descriptions are searchable keywords. Mention React, TypeScript, Next.js, Swift, Claude Code, MCP in the actual text.

### 6. Featured Section

Pin these (in order):
1. **First published article** (once written — "What 472 PRs in 11 Weeks Taught Me")
2. **Resume PDF** — downloadable directly from the profile
3. **Link to robabby.com** — portfolio site
4. **Link to Sherpa consulting site** — establishes the practice

### 7. Recommendations — Get Exactly 3

The sweet spot. Profiles with recommendations are **50% more likely to receive job offers.**

**Who to ask:**
1. A former manager or tech lead (PartySlate era) — validates leadership trust
2. A peer/colleague — confirms collaboration ability
3. A client or cross-functional partner — proves external impact

**Ask them to mention:** specific technologies, leadership, shipping velocity, architectural judgment.

### 8. Activity — Post Weekly Minimum

LinkedIn's algorithm ranks active members higher in recruiter search results. "Active" = posts at least once and comments at least twice per week.

**Content cadence for Rob:**
- Week 1: First article ("What 472 PRs in 11 Weeks Taught Me") as LinkedIn carousel
- Week 2: Engage with agentic engineering discourse (comment on Karpathy/Osmani/Willison posts)
- Week 3: Short post about a specific technical insight from building WavePoint or Sherpa Studio
- Week 4: Carousel about the governance infrastructure approach
- Ongoing: Comment on 2-3 posts per day in the AI engineering space

### 9. Verification Badge

Verified accounts receive **60% more profile views.** Three types available (all free):
- Identity verification (most impactful, processes in minutes)
- Workplace verification
- Education verification

Rob should verify identity at minimum.

## What NOT to Do

- ❌ Don't use the public "Open to Work" green banner at Staff+ level
- ❌ Don't leave the Skills section sparse — fill it aggressively
- ❌ Don't use creative internal titles that aren't searchable ("AI-Augmented Builder" → use "Staff Frontend Engineer" in the title field)
- ❌ Don't write the About section in third person
- ❌ Don't ignore the Featured section — it's prime real estate
- ❌ Don't just optimize and go silent — activity is a ranking signal
