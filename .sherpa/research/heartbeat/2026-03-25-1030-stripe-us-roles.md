## Role 1: Staff Engineer, Experimental Projects (ID: 7280343)

**Fit: 9/10** 🔥
**Salary:** $246,600–$369,800 USD
**URL:** https://stripe.com/jobs/listing/staff-engineer-experimental-projects/7280343

### What it is
Stripe's in-house innovation lab — concept-to-prototype in days. 12+ years required. Autonomy to champion solutions across ambiguous, high-business-impact areas. "Shipping products from scratch" is a core requirement.

**Key preferred qual:** "passion for working on side-projects, open source, or similar self-driven initiatives" — basically a job description of Rob's last 12 months.

### Cover Letter (~340 words)

I've spent the last year building what I couldn't hire out — two production products, solo, from scratch: Sherpa Studio, an agentic engineering workflow suite; and WavePoint, an astrology platform with a Next.js web app, six native Apple apps, and an MCP server. 472 pull requests in 11 weeks. Not because I was trying to set a record, but because I was testing whether one engineer, working with AI as a true collaborator rather than a shortcut, could compress timelines that used to require teams.

The answer is yes, and the Experimental Projects team sounds like the place where that matters.

What I find compelling about this role isn't just the autonomy or the pace — it's the mandate to take promising ideas from ambiguity to working reality, often in days. That's exactly the muscle I've been building. With Sherpa, I designed the infrastructure first, then iterated on real users from day one. With WavePoint, I shipped six Apple apps and a full-stack web platform without a dedicated team, PM, or design resource. Both are live products with real usage, not demo repos.

Before these projects, I spent 13 years as a Staff Frontend Engineer with a background in product engineering across complex systems — not just writing code but identifying which problems were worth solving and leading cross-functional efforts to solve them. I've navigated ambiguity at scale before. What's changed is that I now move faster, with more leverage, and I understand AI-native tooling deeply enough to both use it and build it.

The preferred qualification that mentions a "passion for side-projects" — I'd say it's less a side interest and more a defining trait. I have two production platforms to show for it.

I'd be glad to talk about what I've built and how the Experimental Projects team's mission maps to what I know how to do.

## Role 2: Frontend Platform Engineer, JS Infrastructure (ID: 7743307)

**Fit: 7/10** ⚠️ May be Canada-primary (salary listed in CAD)
**URL:** https://stripe.com/jobs/listing/frontend-platform-engineer-javascript-infrastructure/7743307

### What it is
Build Stripe's package manager, bundler, TypeScript infrastructure at multi-million-line scale. Explicit bullet: **"Lay the groundwork for AI-native frontend development."** Own SLOs for build performance and developer tooling reliability.

### Why it fits (with caveat)
The AI-native development line is a genuine hook. Sherpa Studio IS developer tooling. Rob's TypeScript depth is strong. Slight discount: pure infra (bundlers/build pipelines), not product engineering. CAD salary may signal Canada-preferred.

### Cover Letter (~350 words)

The line in your job description that stood out: "Lay the groundwork for AI-native frontend development." I've been living in that problem for the past year.

Sherpa Studio is an agentic engineering workflow suite I built as a solo engineer — tools, infrastructure, and a consulting practice built around the idea that the edit/build/test loop is fundamentally changing. I spent months building not just on top of AI tools, but thinking about the infrastructure underneath them: how dev workflows need to change, where tooling creates friction, and what it looks like when developer experience genuinely gets out of the way.

That's what drew me to this role. The JavaScript Infrastructure team's mandate — making large-scale frontend development fast, reliable, and maintainable for thousands of engineers — is exactly the kind of high-leverage, upstream problem I find compelling. Fix the foundation, everything downstream improves.

My background is 13+ years of frontend and fullstack engineering, most recently at Staff level. I'm fluent in TypeScript, React, and the modern build ecosystem — not just as a user but with the kind of depth that comes from debugging gnarly issues in complex, real-world codebases. I've shipped production systems at scale, owned significant pieces of technical infrastructure, and communicated architectural decisions clearly across distributed teams.

The thing I'm most proud of isn't any single feature — it's building two full production products solo over the past year (Sherpa and WavePoint, a Next.js platform plus six native Apple apps) in ~11 weeks of net work. That velocity required learning to think about tooling and infrastructure not just as support, but as force multipliers. Investing in the loop compounds.

I'd bring both the frontend depth this role needs and direct experience thinking about how AI changes the developer experience layer — which your JD suggests is where the team is headed.

Happy to talk more about what that looks like in practice.
