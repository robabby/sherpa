# Content Strategy for Rob Abby — AI-Native Engineering Job Search

Date: 2026-03-24

---

## 1. What to Publish First

### Lead with the "472 PRs in 11 weeks" story — framed as a *system*, not a stat

**The hook is the number. The signal is the methodology.**

> **"How I shipped 472 PRs in 11 weeks as a solo engineer — the agentic workflow behind it"**

Structure:
- The problem: building production-quality software solo at pace
- The system: how you orchestrated agents, structured PR flow, handled review/QA
- The tools: which AI tools played which roles, and why
- The trade-offs: where the system broke down or needed human judgment
- The output: what two production products look like as a result

**Secondary pieces to queue:**
1. "Building Sherpa Studio: Lessons from designing an agentic dev workflow product"
2. "One engineer, 6 native Apple apps + a Next.js platform: The WavePoint architecture"
3. "What I'd tell a team about AI-augmented development after 11 weeks of pushing"

## 3. What's Getting Traction Right Now (early 2026)

- "One person built X with AI" stories with concrete output metrics
- MCP deep-dives and integration stories (Rob has a WavePoint MCP server — differentiator)
- System design for agents — multi-agent orchestration, tool use patterns
- Spec-driven development
- Honest, first-person accounts with actual code/numbers/architecture

What's NOT working: Generic "5 AI tools I use" listicles. Hiring managers are tuned out.

---

## 4. LinkedIn Content Strategy

### Cadence: 2-3x per week (quality > volume)

| Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Project proof posts | 1x/week | Demonstrates capability |
| Insight posts (one concrete lesson) | 1x/week | Demonstrates thinking |
| Engagement posts (real decisions) | 1x/2 weeks | Increases reach |
| Availability signal | Once | Sets context |

### Format:
- Short paragraphs, mobile-first
- Lead with the most interesting sentence
- 2-3 hashtags max
- Visuals help (GitHub contribution graph, architecture diagrams)
- Confident and specific tone, not humble-braggy
