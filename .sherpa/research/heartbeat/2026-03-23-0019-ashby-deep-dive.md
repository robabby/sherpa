## Leveling & Compensation (Published Transparently)

This is unusual — Abhik (VP Eng) published full engineering levels and comp bands. Key points:

- **6 levels:** L1 (junior) → L6 (principal/staff)
- **Staff = L5.** Principal = L6. Rob is targeting L4 (Senior) or L5 (Staff).
- **L5 criteria:** Leads roadmap for product areas, creates leverage across the org, operates with minimal direction, reference-checked
- **Comp discussion happens at the first call** — come prepared with a number
- **Equity is meaningful** — they share financial metrics (runway, growth, retention) so candidates can value it

**Comp estimates (derived from SF-area engineering market + Ashby's stated philosophy of generous bands):**
- Senior (L4): ~$160K–$200K base
- Staff (L5): ~$200K–$250K base
- They've described being competitive with "growth-stage startup comp" — not FAANG, but strong

**Rob's target:** Apply for Staff (L5). If they level down to Senior (L4) during screening, ask about timeline to Staff promotion. Their leveling system is structured enough that this is a concrete, answerable question.

## Cover Letter Draft

**Title:** Product Engineer (Staff/Senior) — Americas

---

Benji and Abhik built Ashby because they wanted to do their best work. I spent the last 11 weeks trying the same experiment — and I want to do it inside a team.

I'm a Staff Frontend Engineer who founded two companies and shipped three products in 12 weeks. WavePoint — a Next.js web app and 6 native Apple apps — went from zero to production with 472+ PRs as the sole engineer. I was the PM, designer, frontend engineer, backend engineer, iOS engineer, and AI infrastructure engineer. No daily standups. No sprint planning. Just features, shipped.

I built that velocity on the Sherpa framework: a behavioral governance system for AI-assisted development with 91+ React workflow components, an MCP server, and structured initiative lifecycles. The system exists because I wanted to stop being a "JIRA jockey" — I wanted to own the outcome, not the ticket.

Your engineering description says "consistently creates leverage with high velocity, minimal direction, and without compromising quality." The 472 PRs are the output. The governance infrastructure is how I maintained quality while moving that fast.

The stack (TypeScript, React, GraphQL, Node.js) is home territory. Your leveling transparency is exactly what I want going into a conversation — I'll come prepared with a number.

I'd love to swap in the frontend interview track if available. Happy to walk through anything from architecture trade-offs to a live coding session on a problem you're actually working on.

[Signature]
