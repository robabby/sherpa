## Variant 1 — Cultural Mirror

**Subject:** Staff Software Engineer, Product Engineering — Rob Abby

---

Ashby's VP of Engineering described the ideal Product Engineer as someone who "conceives, implements, and ships features end-to-end with minimal direction." For the past 11 weeks, that's exactly what I did — twice over.

I'm Rob Abby, a Staff Software Engineer with 13+ years of experience. I recently built two production platforms as sole engineer: **Sherpa Studio**, an agentic engineering workflow suite with an AI consulting framework, and **WavePoint**, an astrology platform comprising a full Next.js web app, six native Apple apps, and an MCP server. 472 pull requests. Two shipped products. Zero meetings to coordinate.

That's not a humble brag — it's a data point about how I work. I don't need a lot of direction to move fast. I write my own specs, make design tradeoffs, build the infrastructure, and ship. I thrive in the kind of environment you've described: high ownership, low ceremony, real output.

What draws me to Ashby specifically is that the philosophy isn't just rhetoric. The almost-no-meeting culture, engineers owning features from conception to launch, the radically transparent comp approach — these aren't perks, they're the conditions I do my best work in. I've watched Notion, Linear, and Shopify build exceptional products; the fact that they trust Ashby to support that process says something real about the craft here.

My stack maps cleanly to yours: TypeScript, React, Node.js, GraphQL. I've spent the last year deep in AI-native architecture — not just using AI tools but building the tooling, the agents, the workflows, and the products around them.

I'd welcome the chance to go deeper on any of this — or to jump straight into a technical conversation. I'm also happy to take the frontend-focused interview track if that's a better fit for how you evaluate Product Engineers.

Thanks for your time.

**Rob Abby**
