## Key Takeaway

This is the most visually-creative role in the pipeline — the Design Engineering team owns "the highest level of polish, creativity and interaction on Vercel." It's also the one where Rob's aesthetic judgment (WavePoint's "Modern Mystic" design language, cross-platform consistency, animations) is most directly relevant. The honest question to ask before applying: does Rob want to be a design engineer at a marketing-adjacent team, or does he want to be a product engineer? Both are valid — they're just different. If the answer is yes to Vercel, this is the right role.

### What the Role Actually Is

This is not a product engineering role — it's a **design-engineering hybrid focused on acquisition surfaces**: vercel.com, events, marketing pages. Think: the thing that makes a visitor go "wow, this company is excellent" before they sign up.

Key responsibilities:
- Own vercel.com and marketing surfaces from a design + engineering perspective
- CMS integrations (Payload CMS), analytics (Rudderstack, Clearbit, Sequel.io)
- Core Web Vitals, SEO, accessibility — the full frontend craft stack
- Design systems contribution — feedback and iteration to empower the whole company
- Mentorship: "advocate for and mentor other design engineers"
- Creative license: "Designers at Vercel have broad creative license"

## The Design vs. Product Tension

This role is genuinely design-engineering, not pure product engineering. The surfaces are acquisition/marketing, not the product itself. That means:

**Upside for Rob:**
- Enormous creative autonomy — "broad creative license"
- Direct impact visible to millions (vercel.com is one of the most-visited dev sites)
- Mentorship + systems work — not just execution
- Vercel brand is a strong career credential

**Potential tension:**
- Less product ownership than Ashby/Linear/Level.io
- Marketing surfaces are slower to iterate than product surfaces
- The "Am I a JIRA jockey?" risk is lower here but the "Am I primarily serving marketing?" risk is present

**My assessment:** Worth applying if Rob genuinely loves the craft of building beautiful, performant acquisition experiences. If he's most energized by feature velocity and product ownership, Ashby or Linear are better daily-work fits.

## Cover Letter Draft

**Role:** Design Engineer (Senior/Staff) — United States

---

Vercel is the platform I've been building on for the past year. I'd like to help build the platform itself.

I'm a Staff Frontend Engineer who spent the last 12 weeks building a full production Next.js app — a web app and 6 native Apple apps — solo, with 472+ PRs shipped. The design system I built for it, called "Modern Mystic," required designing and engineering a consistent visual language across web (Radix UI, Tailwind, custom animations) and native (SwiftUI) surfaces from scratch. No designer to hand off from. No component library to inherit. Just: what does this product need to feel like, and how do I make it real in code?

That end-to-end design-to-implementation ownership is what I understand the Design Engineering team does for Vercel — at the highest level of polish, for the surfaces that introduce the world to Vercel.

Before founding, I spent 6 years at PartySlate (first developer through Series B) where I built a 25+ component design system serving 200k+ monthly users, led Core Web Vitals improvements across the platform, and shipped high-craft, performant UI across a consumer-facing marketplace. The craft bar was high because our product was competing for the attention of event planners who could just go back to Google.

The Design Engineering role at Vercel sits at an intersection I find genuinely exciting: Next.js expertise, design systems thinking, creative autonomy, and measurable acquisition impact. I've built on that intersection. I'd like to own it at Vercel's scale.

I'm fully remote-eligible (Bellingham, WA). Happy to share portfolio work and go deep on anything from animation approaches to Core Web Vitals strategy in the interview.

---

**Word count:** ~270. Leads with the Next.js credential, pivots to design-to-implementation ownership (which is the job), then bridges to PartySlate for scale and craft credibility.
