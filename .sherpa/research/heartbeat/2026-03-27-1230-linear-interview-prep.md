# Linear Interview Prep — March 27, 2026

## Interview Format: Paid Work Trial

- **Duration:** 2-5 days (typically 3-5 for engineering)
- **Structure:** Work on a real project with the Linear team, using actual internal tools
- **Compensation:** Fully paid (industry standard daily rate)
- **What they evaluate:** Ability to ship real features to production, collaboration, product thinking, quality of execution
- **Universal:** Everyone from engineers to C-level goes through work trials
- **Success rate:** 96% retention over 4 years for hired candidates
- **Example:** One engineer (Kenneth Skovhus, 2023) shipped a new database entity, GraphQL resolver, and UI by day 3

## Tech Stack

- **Frontend:** React, TypeScript, MobX
- **Backend:** Node.js, PostgreSQL, GraphQL, Apollo
- **Infrastructure:** Google Cloud (Kubernetes, Cloud SQL, Pub/Sub), Docker, Redis
- **Tools:** GitHub, VS Code, Retool

## What They Value

1. **"Passion for the craft of building software"** — #1 hiring criterion
2. Quality over speed — product quality > velocity
3. Product thinking — engineers are "de-facto PMs" who make product decisions
4. Full-stack mindset — no rigid handoffs
5. Shared responsibility for quality across entire team

- **No silos:** Connected teams, everyone shares responsibility for quality
- **Taste-driven:** Decisions based on opinion and taste, not A/B tests or heavy metrics
- **No traditional PM roles:** Product duties distributed across engineering/design
- **No durable cross-functional teams:** Teams assemble around projects then disperse
- **Profitable & efficient:** Negative lifetime burn rate, 0.5% of revenue on marketing
- **AI-forward customers:** 66% of Forbes top 50 AI companies use Linear
