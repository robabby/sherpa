## Key Takeaway

The AI consulting market in 2026 is underpriced relative to demand. Only 6% of organizations using AI qualify as "high performers" — the implementation gap is the opportunity. Rob's strongest positioning isn't as a generic AI consultant; it's as an engineer who built behavioral governance infrastructure at production scale and can implement it for teams. That specificity justifies premium rates and shapes which three products to lead with on the Sherpa site.

## Why Hourly Billing Is Wrong for Sherpa (The Jonathan Stark Case)

Jonathan Stark's core thesis: hourly billing **caps your income by your hours** and **penalizes expertise** (faster = less revenue). The alternative: productized services and value-based pricing.

**Hourly problems:**
- If Rob solves a client's governance problem in 2 hours because he's built the exact system before, he earns $400–$600. A junior would bill 20 hours for $2K–$3K and do worse work.
- Clients can't budget reliably, which slows sales cycles
- Scope creep is constant, creating relationship friction

**Value-based pricing logic:**
- "What is implementing a behavioral governance framework worth to your team?" → "If it saves 20 hours/week per engineer and you have 5 engineers..." → the value is $200K+/year
- Even a modest share of that value justifies $15K–$25K for an implementation engagement
- The price is set by the outcome, not the hours

**The transition path:**
- Start with fixed-scope productized services (clear deliverable, published price)
- Move to monthly retainers for ongoing clients
- Introduce value-based pricing as you accumulate ROI data from case studies
- WavePoint is the first case study — use it to establish the ROI narrative

## Recommended Engagement Model: Three Products

### Product 1: Agentic Workflow Audit — $2,500

**The gateway product.** Borrowed directly from PocketCTO's $999 audit model, priced slightly higher to reflect AI governance specificity.

**What it is:** A structured 1-week engagement that maps the team's current AI development workflow, identifies governance gaps, and delivers a written report with specific recommendations.

**Deliverables:**
- 1-hour discovery call (current workflow, tools, team structure, pain points)
- Review of existing AGENTS.md / conventions / prompting patterns (or lack thereof)
- Written report: what's working, what's breaking, specific gaps
- Prioritized recommendation list with implementation order
- 30-min readout call

**Why this price:** $2,500 is under most client approval thresholds, making the sales cycle fast. It's a no-brainer "let's start here" for teams spending $10K+/month on AI tooling but not getting predictable output. The audit almost always leads to an implementation engagement.

**Why this works for Rob:** Creates client relationship before a larger commitment. Surfaces exactly what each client needs. Positions Rob as credible before asking for a bigger budget.

**How to present it on the site:**
> "Not sure where to start? The Agentic Workflow Audit is a 1-week engagement that maps your team's AI development workflow, identifies governance gaps, and delivers a written report with specific recommendations. Fixed scope. Fixed price. Useful either way."

---

### Product 2: AI Engineering Sprint — $15,000–$20,000

**The core engagement.** 4-week fixed-scope delivery of 1–2 production-ready agentic systems.

**What it is:** Rob implements a specific, scoped agentic engineering initiative — typically behavioral governance infrastructure, MCP tooling, or an AI-augmented workflow for a specific development task.

**Deliverables (example: governance implementation):**
- AGENTS.md system designed for their codebase and team norms
- Behavioral constraint framework with auto-loading context
- Initiative lifecycle templates (planning → implementation → review)
- MCP server or tool integration for the highest-value agent action (if applicable)
- Documentation for team adoption
- 2 follow-up calls post-delivery for questions

**Why this price:** $15K is well within a "single engineer for a month" budget comparison (a senior engineer at $200K/year costs ~$17K/month in salary alone, before taxes and benefits). Rob delivers in 4 weeks what would take an internal team 3–6 months to figure out on their own.

**Pricing flexibility:**
- $15K for a clean, well-defined scope (clear codebase, good documentation, 1-2 engineers)
- $20K for messier environments (legacy code, multiple frameworks, 5+ engineers)
- Add 20% if the client wants a second month of "embed and iterate" after delivery

---

### Product 3: Fractional AI Engineering Partner — $6,000–$10,000/month

**The ongoing relationship.** Monthly retainer for teams that want continuous improvement rather than a one-time implementation.

**What it is:** Rob is the team's AI engineering advisor and implementer — on call for architectural questions, reviews new AI tooling decisions, iterates on governance as the codebase evolves, and runs one focused implementation initiative per quarter.

**What's included at $6K/month:**
- 10–15 hours/month of dedicated time
- Architecture and tool selection guidance (async)
- Monthly review of AI workflow health + governance drift
- Quarterly mini-sprint: one focused improvement implemented
- Slack/async access during business hours

**What's included at $10K/month:**
- 20–25 hours/month
- All of the above plus hands-on implementation work
- Fortnightly check-in calls
- Access to Sherpa Studio tooling (if/when productized for external use)

**Target client:** A Series A–B company with 4–10 engineers, actively using Claude Code or Cursor, struggling with consistency/drift as the team grows. They know they need governance but don't have the expertise in-house.

## Pricing Philosophy for the Sherpa Site

**Publish the prices.** The PocketCTO model is instructive: "Most firms won't tell you what they charge. You click pricing and get a calendar link. I find this annoying as a buyer and pointless as a seller."

Publishing prices differentiates Sherpa from every VC-backed AI consultancy that hides costs. It signals:
- Confidence (you know your value)
- Respect for the client's time (no 3-call sales process for a $2,500 engagement)
- Alignment with Sherpa's foundation stone value: Honesty

**Where to put them:** The Services page on sherpa.consulting. Each product gets its own block with:
- What it is (1 sentence)
- Who it's for (1 sentence)
- What you get (3–5 bullets)
- Price (prominently displayed)
- "Get started" button → booking link or intake form

## Target Client Profiles

### Ideal Client 1: Well-funded Startup with AI Workflow Pain

- **Size:** 5–20 engineers, Series A–B
- **Problem:** Using Claude Code or Cursor but output quality is inconsistent; no governance; different engineers have different prompting conventions; AI-generated code doesn't match architectural standards
- **Budget:** $15K–$50K project budgets; $5K–$10K/month retainer
- **Decision maker:** CTO or VP Engineering
- **How to reach:** LinkedIn (hiring for "AI engineer" or "senior frontend"), AI Tinkerers events, referrals from other clients

### Ideal Client 2: Engineering Team That Just Adopted Claude Code

- **Size:** 3–10 engineers
- **Problem:** Excited about Claude Code but worried about consistency; no AGENTS.md; agents making unexpected architectural decisions
- **Budget:** $2,500–$15K initial; may grow
- **Decision maker:** CTO or founder-engineer
- **Entry point:** Agentic Workflow Audit → implementation sprint

### Ideal Client 3: Product Agency Scaling AI Output

- **Size:** 5–15 engineers delivering for multiple clients
- **Problem:** AI tooling is accelerating individual work but creating quality inconsistency across the team; client deliverables lack the architectural coherence expected
- **Budget:** $10K–$25K project; $6K+/month retainer
- **Entry point:** Custom sprint proposal based on their workflow

## Pricing Evolution Path

**Now (no case studies yet):**
Lead with the Audit ($2,500) as the entry point. Don't publish the Sprint or Retainer prices prominently — say "contact to scope" and set them in conversation where you can establish value first.

**After 2–3 completed audits (3–6 months):**
Publish all three prices. Add testimonials and the WavePoint case study. The data exists to justify them.

**After 1 completed Sprint (6–12 months):**
Add a "What outcomes have our clients seen?" section. Begin introducing value-based pricing for Sprint-level engagements — price relative to engineer-hours saved, not weeks of Rob's time.

**Long term:**
3–4 monthly retainer clients = $18K–$40K/month in predictable revenue = the consulting income target without employment.
