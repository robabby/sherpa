## Company Overview

Vercel is the platform behind Next.js, v0, and the AI SDK. They position themselves as the "AI Cloud" — infrastructure for shipping AI-native web apps at scale. ~800 employees, valued at $9.3B as of Oct 2025.

**CEO:** Guillermo Rauch — deeply technical, Next.js creator, open-source native, frequently writes and speaks about the frontend/AI frontier. Recently brought in Sébastien Chopin (NuxtLabs founder) — Nuxt now on the platform.

**Company narrative in 2026:** "2026 is the year of agents." v0 just relaunched (Feb 2026) as a production-grade tool — GitHub import, branch/PR workflows, secure DB integrations, enterprise security. Next milestone: end-to-end agentic workflows + self-driving infra (the cloud that observes, reasons, and auto-optimizes).

## Tech Stack

Vercel's own products run on Vercel, naturally. Key signals:

- **Framework:** Next.js (they own it) — App Router, Server Components, Streaming SSR
- **Language:** TypeScript everywhere
- **AI:** Vercel AI SDK (open source) — their own toolkit, used by millions of devs
- **Edge Runtime:** V8 Isolates at the edge, Node.js on server (and now fast-webstreams lib for perf)
- **CMS/Analytics integrations mentioned in Design Eng JD:** Payload CMS, Rudderstack, Clearbit, Sequel.io
- **Infra:** Custom CDN, serverless functions, edge network (not AWS-native despite partnership)
- **OSS culture:** Most core tooling is open source (Next.js, AI SDK, fast-webstreams, Turbo, SWC)
- **Design systems:** Internal design system, Core Web Vitals obsession, Accessibility-first

Key engineering philosophy signals from the eng blog:
- Deep perf work at the runtime level (WebStreams 10x optimization, published Feb 2026)
- AI-driven test-driven reimplementation (used AI to rewrite WebStreams spec-compliant from test suite)
- Benchmark-driven engineering culture

## Culture & Interview

**Culture signals:**
- Historically "LFG" energy — ship fast, teams moved quickly. Some Glassdoor commentary (late 2025) notes a shift toward "operating like Stripe/Google" post-Series F. Growing pains of scaling from ~series B mindset to enterprise posture.
- Strong OSS culture — open-source contribution is table stakes, not a nice-to-have. JDs explicitly call it out.
- Design Engineer team has "broad creative license" and leads design-driven projects. High craft bar.
- "You can just ship things" is their internal mission statement for employees — autonomy-forward.
- In-office anchor days Mon/Tue/Fri for those within commute radius; fully remote otherwise.

**Interview process signals:**
- Vercel historically doesn't hire like traditional companies — they look for strong open-source profiles and real shipped work.
- Design Eng and DX roles likely involve portfolio/work sample components.
- Technical bar is high — eng blog content (WebStreams perf deep-dive) signals the team thinks in systems and benchmarks.

**Compensation (AI SDK eng as benchmark):**
- SF: $196K–$294K base + equity + benefits
- Remote/non-SF likely adjusted down somewhat but still competitive
