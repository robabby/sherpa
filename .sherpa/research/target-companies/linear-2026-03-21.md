## Company Overview

**Linear** (linear.app) is a product development platform founded in 2019 by three Finnish engineers: Karri Saarinen (CEO), Jori Lallo (CPO), and Tuomas Artman (CTO). They describe themselves as building "a new species of product tool" — purpose-built for speed, focus, and craftsmanship.

- **Users:** 25,000+ companies including OpenAI, Coinbase, Ramp, CashApp, Scale AI, Cursor, Boom, and Mercury
- **Team size:** ~99 people across 15+ countries, 10+ time zones
- **Funding:** $134M total, latest being $82M Series C (June 2025, led by Accel) at **$1.25B valuation**
- **Profitable:** Operates profitably — no growth hacking, no A/B tests, slow intentional hiring
- **Mission:** "Inspire and accelerate builders." Currently pivoting toward agent-native product workflows.

The company is entering what they call the "next shift" — embedding AI deeply into product workflows, enabling agents to work alongside human teams inside Linear. This is a generational bet they're executing on right now, and it's the primary reason they're hiring engineers.

## Culture & Interview Process

### Culture (from README + Remote Work article + Careers page)

**11 Core Principles:**
1. Ship early — simplify and ship smaller
2. Build with users — listen carefully and understand their problems
3. Know what good looks like — find and create good things
4. Think in principles — not in playbooks
5. Build things that last — don't make it a future person's problem
6. Avoid side quests — don't fix every problem
7. Say it as it is — get to the point
8. Keep the team small, do more with less
9. Hire the best people
10. Create fans
11. Fully remote

**Remote philosophy (Karri, Oct 2025):**
- 99-person team, 15+ countries, 10+ time zones
- Small teams: 2-4 people, clear ownership, project leads rotate
- One product team, one roadmap — no OKRs, no A/B tests
- "Deep thinking time is treated as something worth protecting"
- Zero-bugs policy with SLAs
- Quality Wednesdays — team trains to see what's off (1,000+ small fixes over 2 years)
- Annual company offsite (Copenhagen 2025, previously Mexico City, Helsinki, Lisbon)
- Coworking hubs in Berlin, NYC, SF (optional)
- Monthly 1-hour all-hands

**Equity:**
- 10-year exercise window
- Early exercise in US
- Refreshers over time
- "Linear Infinity" — paths to liquidity with recharge sabbaticals

### Interview Process

**Standard process (from work trials post + Glassdoor):**
1. Intro call with recruiter
2. Hiring manager interview
3. Role-relevant skills assessments with team members (panel of 3-5 people)
4. Panel voting (strong no → strong yes scores)
5. **Work Trial** — 2-5 paid days working on a real project

**Work trials (the signature Linear process):**
- Paid daily rate
- Real project, not contrived
- Access to Slack, Notion, Linear, GitHub, Figma
- Has a kick-off meeting, check-in, final presentation
- Candidate leads check-ins (testing ownership and initiative)
- 96% retention rate after 4 years of work trials
- "Anything other than a strong yes is a no"

**What they assess:**
- Product sense and taste
- Self-direction and initiative
- Communication and ownership
- How they work in a remote startup environment
- Decision-making in ambiguous situations

**Glassdoor signal (2020 early interview):** Karri himself asked about "biggest achievements in engineering that showcase excellence craftsmanship." This is still their operating mode.

**Key advice:** Come in with a specific, impressive story. Not "I built features" — "I shipped X in Y days, here's the tradeoff I made." They respond to craftsmanship narratives.
