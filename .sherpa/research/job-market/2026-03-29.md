## 📊 Market Signals

### Demand Patterns (March 2026)
- **"AI-native" as baseline expectation:** HN March 2026 thread explicitly calls out "uses AI as a core part of their workflow" as a requirement even at early-stage companies. This is now table stakes.
- **Agentic experience is differentiating:** PostHog and Pocus are specifically calling out shipped agentic AI experience — Rob's Sherpa + WavePoint work is directly relevant.
- **Staff-level React/Next.js still strong:** 343+ remote Next.js roles active, 5,500+ NextJS/React remote roles on Indeed alone.
- **Product engineer framing growing:** The "product engineer" title (vs. "software engineer") is appearing more at craft-focused companies — Linear, Ashby, Buffer. Signals companies that want engineers who think about the whole user experience, not just tickets.
- **Design Engineer niche active:** Vercel is the canonical home, but this hybrid role is emerging elsewhere. Rob's UI craft gives him an edge here.
- **Staff Engineers salary range:** HN March 2026 shows Staff Software Engineer (Ruby+React stack) at $200–250K range. Linear/Airbnb staff roles likely $220–300K total comp.

### Industry Trends
- Claude Code named as "most loved" AI coding tool among Director+ engineers (Pragmatic Engineer, ~1 month ago).
- Cursor at 19% adoption, GitHub Copilot at 9% — AI-assisted development now assumed.
- Companies that value software craftsmanship (Linear, Ashby, Vercel) remain competitive on comp and culture.
