# Job Market Scan — March 28, 2026

## TL;DR

The market for senior/staff engineers who can work AI-natively is **hot and getting hotter**. The emerging breakout title is **Forward-Deployed Engineer (FDE)** — essentially what Rob has been building toward with Sherpa. AI/ML posting volumes jumped 163% in 2025 YoY. Remote frontend specifically is thin (~2-5 listings on RemoteOK at any given time) but **quality roles exist** at the right companies. The gap between "AI tool user" and "AI-native builder" is now the primary hiring filter at top companies.

---

## Market Signals

### 📈 Macro Trends (Q1 2026)

- **AI/ML roles**: 49,200 postings in 2025, up **163% from 2024** (Robert Half)
- **AI employer job postings** up **117% from 2024→2025** overall
- **Remote frontend** thin on volume but **$60K–$180K** salary range (likely underrepresenting senior comp)
- **Remote AI engineer** comp: **$130K–$300K+ total** (median $220K, per levels.fyi data)
- **Staff SWE at AI companies**: trending toward $250K–$350K+ TC
- Biggest hiring challenges: soft skills + real production AI experience (not just tooling familiarity)

### 🔥 Hottest Emerging Role: Forward-Deployed Engineer

Business Insider (this week) called FDE "the hottest job in AI." Key profile:
- Bridges AI models and real business workflows
- Technical depth + product/customer-facing communication
- Less lab work, more delivery
- "Experimentation isn't a phase, it's the job" (Augment Code's blog post, 2 weeks ago)

**This is a near-perfect match for Rob's positioning** — Sherpa consulting practice, production AI experience, full-stack builder with Swift + Next.js + agentic tooling.

### The "2026 Frontend" Framing

OnlyFrontendJobs.com published "Is Frontend Dead in 2026?" — conclusion: no, but the bar shifted:
> "Top companies want frontend engineers who can design an API contract, write basic BFF layers, and push an update through CI/CD via Vercel or Docker."

The dividing line is no longer framework fluency — it's **architecture judgment + AI-augmented velocity**. Resume tip going around: listing "Claude Code" or "GitHub Copilot" as skills is the new "Microsoft Word on a resume." What matters: architecture decisions, outcomes, AI-assisted speed with engineering-grade quality.
