## Summary

- **Industry verticals matter more in 2026:** Buyers are no longer just looking for "an AI consultant" — they're looking for someone who understands their domain. Finance/banking, healthcare, and retail dominate enterprise spend, but professional services (law, accounting) and creator economy verticals are wide open for specialized solos.
- **Washington State is a legitimate AI ecosystem:** #6 nationally, #5 in startup activity, and Seattle is the #2 AI job hub behind only SF Bay Area. 95% of state activity is concentrated in Seattle metro — but that concentration means Bellingham-based consultants have outsized access to a major market without SF cost of living.
- **Bellingham has a niche identity:** Agriculture tech, maritime, and rural health AI are the native strengths. Local startups like Cascadia AI Agents (agentic workflows for professional services), Carbon Robotics ($150M+ funded AgTech), and Lighthouse Health AI represent the ecosystem. For Sherpa, the local angle is less "geographic" and more "credibility signal" — Rob is embedded in one of the strongest regional ecosystems outside the Bay.
- **Vertical specialization is the solo consultant's moat.** Generic "AI consulting" is commoditizing. Specialists in specific domains (legal automation, healthcare workflows, SaaS product velocity) command premium rates and shorter sales cycles.

## Key Findings

### Top Verticals Buying AI Consulting in 2026

**Finance & Banking — 22.3% market share (largest single sector)**
- Use cases: fraud detection, risk management, customer service automation, regulatory compliance
- 80%+ of global banks already use AI-powered chatbots/fraud tools — market is mature *for the big firms*, but community banks, credit unions, fintech startups are underserved
- NLP for compliance, credit risk automation, customer engagement personalization — specific high-value niches

**Healthcare — Fast-growing, high regulatory complexity**
- Clinical documentation automation, diagnostic support, patient workflow optimization
- Rural healthcare is especially underserved; Bellingham's Lighthouse Health AI is a local example
- HIPAA compliance complexity = premium rates for consultants who understand it

**Retail & E-Commerce**
- Personalization engines, inventory optimization, customer experience AI
- Mid-size DTC brands ($5M–$50M revenue) are the sweet spot — too small for enterprise solutions, too large to DIY
- Churn prediction systems: $20K–$60K project; sales forecasting: $25K–$80K

**Manufacturing**
- Supply chain optimization, predictive maintenance
- Bellingham-adjacent: Port of Bellingham logistics/trade corridor is an active niche (Northwest Predictive startup working this angle)

**Professional Services (LAW, ACCOUNTING) — UNDERSERVED NICHE**
- Cascadia AI Agents (Bellingham) is building exactly this: agentic workflows for contract analysis, regulatory filing, compliance checks for small law and accounting firms
- This vertical has: strong willingness to pay, clear ROI (time saved = billable hours), and almost zero credible solo competition
- **High-signal opportunity for Sherpa:** Rob's technical depth + clean implementation track record = credible entry point into professional services AI

**Creator Economy / Media / Spirituality**
- Almost no specialized solo consultants serving this space
- WavePoint is Rob's unfair advantage here — he has deep credibility in spirituality/wellness tech that no other AI consultant can match
- Use cases: content pipeline automation, audience personalization, multi-platform publishing workflows, AI-enhanced product development
- Community apps, meditation/wellness platforms, astrology/spirituality apps: this niche has real money (WavePoint proves it) and zero AI consulting competition

### Vertical AI: The Solo Consultant's Moat

Bessemer Venture Partners' "Building Vertical AI" playbook (Jan 2026) articulates exactly what's happening in consulting:

> "Starting with ancillary tasks — legal research, clinical documentation, construction estimates — offers clear advantages: less resistance to change, faster sales cycles, and obvious ROI that frees up capacity for higher-value work."

This applies directly to consulting positioning. The consultants winning in 2026 are not selling "AI strategy" — they're selling **one specific workflow improvement** with a clear before/after story.

**The pattern that's working:**
1. Pick a vertical where you have some domain knowledge or adjacent credibility
2. Identify the 2–3 highest-pain workflows in that vertical
3. Build a repeatable implementation for those workflows
4. Turn that into a productized engagement with defined scope and outcome
