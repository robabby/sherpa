## 3. Content Formats Performing Well — Educational / Depth-Focused

### Written (Substack/Beehiiv)
- **Long-form essay** (1,500–4,000 words) with personal narrative anchor + universal insight — the gold standard for paid growth
- **"Here's what I learned from X" format** — experience → lesson → framework
- **Series / structured arcs** — recurring formats and themed series build return audience and subscription intent
- **Personal letters** — intimate, direct address, high open rates

### Video (YouTube)
- **Algorithm shift in 2026: watch time > click-through rate** — favors longer, deeper content
- **Long-form (20–40 min) proves expertise and drives course/product sales** — educational channels see 35% higher conversion rates
- **Shorts (45–90 sec)** as discovery funnel feeding into long-form — *not* standalone strategy for depth creators
- **Structured arcs + recurring formats** — viewers return for a format, not just a topic
- **High-comment content wins** — YouTube is amplifying videos that generate real conversation (ask questions, create tension)

### Podcast
- **Video podcasts are now table stakes** — audio-only is a secondary format
- **Beehiiv just launched native podcast hosting** (April 2, 2026 — TechCrunch/Semafor) — one platform for newsletter + podcast + video is now possible
- **Repurposing workflow** is the 2026 efficiency unlock: record once → long-form YouTube → audio podcast → Shorts clips → newsletter essay → Notes posts
