### The Senior/Staff AI-Capable Engineer Premium (new data today)

From Second Talent's 2026 engineering talent report:
- Junior full-stack demand: **DOWN 42%** vs prior year
- ML Engineer demand: **UP 156%**, salaries UP 35%
- Senior Backend demand: **UP 23%**, salaries UP 18%
- **LLM Engineer demand: UP 890%** (new role category, didn't exist 2 years ago)

Key insight for Rob's positioning: **92% of developers now use AI tools** (GitHub data). But the value accrues to those who can architect systems that use AI, not those who passively use Copilot for autocomplete. Rob didn't just use AI tools — he built the infrastructure layer (Sherpa + Sherpa Studio) that his own AI-assisted dev workflow runs on. That puts him in the top percentile of the "AI-capable senior engineer" tier that is currently experiencing the highest salary growth.

**Practical implication:** When Rob negotiates, he should frame his AI-assisted 472-PR output not as "productivity," but as architectural proof that he knows how to build and operate AI-augmented engineering systems — which is exactly what the high-demand roles above are paying for.

- Per the Seattle salary guide (March 2026): backend and full-stack engineers earn $10K–$25K more than frontend-only at equivalent seniority in Seattle
- **Mitigation:** Rob's WavePoint work includes Swift native apps (6 apps), some API integration, and Sherpa Studio (full-stack tooling) — he's closer to "full-stack" than a pure frontend label suggests. Framing matters.
