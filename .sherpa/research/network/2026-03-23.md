## LinkedIn Strategy

LinkedIn is an underutilized asset for Rob right now. Two high-leverage moves this week:

### Move 1: Enable "Open to Work" (Recruiters Only) + Optimize Profile

- Set "Open to Work" visibility to **recruiters only** (not public green banner)
- Increases InMail response rates by ~40% with zero public signal of desperation
- The catch: this only works if the profile is strong. Rob's headline should be specific, not generic.
  - Bad: "Software Engineer | Open to Opportunities"
  - Good: "Staff Frontend Engineer | Building AI-Assisted Products | React, Next.js, Agentic Workflows | Bellingham/Seattle"
- Add specific job titles in preferences: "Staff Frontend Engineer," "Senior Frontend Engineer," "Principal Frontend Engineer" — not just "Frontend Engineer"
- **Action:** 30 minutes. Set Open to Work (recruiters only) + rewrite headline + update About section with 472-PR proof point.

### Move 2: Write One LinkedIn Post This Week

The AI tooling conversation on LinkedIn is hot right now (LinkedIn's own data shows AI Engineer as a top-growing role in 2026). Rob's story is differentiated and compelling.

**Post concept for this week:**
> "I shipped 472 pull requests in 11 weeks using AI agents. Not 472 lines of code — 472 pull requests across a 6-app suite. Here's what that actually looks like, and what broke at velocity 300."

- This kind of post, posted with specificity and honesty (not hype), routinely gets 10K–50K impressions in the engineering community
- **Target audience:** Hiring managers and senior engineers who are trying to understand what "AI-assisted development" looks like in practice
- It surfaces Rob to exactly the people who are evaluating candidates for roles where this skill matters
- **Format:** 3-4 short paragraphs. Specific numbers. Honest about tradeoffs. No "5 lessons" listicle format — real practitioners hate that.
- **Action:** Write and post this week. This is the seed for the long-form case study (which becomes the blog post that opens all the other doors).
