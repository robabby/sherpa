# Design Principles

Seven principles for making UX and content decisions. Each frames a tension — what we lean toward versus what we avoid. When uncertain, return to these.

---

## 1. Grounded over Grandiose

AI content drifts into breathless hype. We stay rooted. Language is precise. Claims are specific. The work speaks for itself.

**In practice:**
- "91% of C-suite execs admit faking AI knowledge" — not "AI is transforming everything"
- Specific outcomes and methods — not vague "AI strategy"
- Name what the framework actually does — not "revolutionary governance solution"
- The Headline Test applies with maximum force to consulting copy

---

## 2. Invitation over Instruction

We offer, we don't prescribe. Organizations bring their own context. We create space for adoption rather than telling people what to think.

**In practice:**
- "You may find that governance reduces pilot failure" — not "You need governance now"
- Show the framework, explain the principles, let teams decide
- Consulting pages suggest possibilities — not mandates
- "Use the framework on your own, or bring us in. Either way works."

---

## 3. Quiet Confidence

Good work doesn't announce itself. Subtle design over flashy. Elegant restraint. The gold accent is earned, not everywhere.

**In practice:**
- Reserve animation for meaningful moments (hero entrance, scroll-reveal)
- No particle effects, 3D globes, or parallax — gravitas over spectacle
- Let case study results speak instead of claiming expertise
- If everything is emphasized, nothing is

---

## 4. Respect the Intelligence

Our audience is thoughtful people navigating real complexity. We don't oversimplify or hedge everything with disclaimers. We trust them to hold nuance.

**In practice:**
- Explain the governance model, not why governance matters
- Skip "As you may know..." preambles
- Present context, let them conclude
- Error messages that inform, not coddle

---

## 5. Guide, Not Guru

We're experienced practitioners who share what we've learned, not authorities who pronounce truth. The consulting relationship is a partnership, not a power dynamic. The goal is to leave people more capable, not more dependent.

**In practice:**
- "Here's what we've seen work" — not "Here's what you must do"
- Share the framework openly — don't gatekeep knowledge behind consulting fees
- Acknowledge what we don't know and where the edges are
- Use behavioral constraints in agent roles, not identity claims — this applies to our own brand too
- Frame consulting as acceleration, not dependency — "we make ourselves unnecessary"
- Never use vendor lock-in language — the framework is yours after we leave
- Sovereignty over subscription: clients own their governance system, not a seat on our platform

---

## 6. Craft over Content

Quality over quantity. One well-written article beats three rushed ones. Better to have five polished pages than twenty thin ones.

**In practice:**
- Ship less, but ship it right
- Polish before proliferating
- Say no to content bloat
- Depth over breadth

---

## 7. Show the Work

We built the framework we use. The best proof of our approach is making it visible — open-source code, public research, transparent methodology. The community that forms around this work is a constituency, not an audience.

**In practice:**
- Research iterations become public thought leadership
- The framework is open-source, not a black box
- Case studies show the real process, not a sanitized narrative
- "We use the same tools we sell" is a structural claim, not a tagline
- Talk about contributors as peers, not "users" — they improve the framework too
- Shared learning over proprietary insight — what we discover, we publish

---

## Applying These Principles

When facing a design or content decision:

1. **Identify the tension** — What are the competing options?
2. **Check the principles** — Which ones apply?
3. **Choose accordingly** — Let the principle resolve the trade-off

Example: "Should we add impressive stats and logos to the homepage?"
- Check: Grounded over Grandiose says only if they're real and specific
- Check: Quiet Confidence says let results speak
- Decision: Real stats with sources, no inflated claims

Example: "Should we gate framework docs behind a lead form?"
- Check: Guide Not Guru says no — share knowledge openly
- Check: Show the Work says make it visible
- Decision: Docs are public, no gate

Example: "Should we publish a half-finished blog post to maintain cadence?"
- Check: Craft over Content says no
- Decision: Wait until it's ready
