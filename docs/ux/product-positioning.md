# Product Positioning

## Sherpa

A behavioral governance framework for agentic workflows — and the consulting team that built it.

**Tagline:** Ship with confidence.

*This document follows [April Dunford's five-component positioning structure](https://www.aprildunford.com/post/a-quickstart-guide-to-positioning). See [Messaging Framework](./messaging-framework.md) for how positioning flows into narrative and per-persona messaging.*

---

## The Name

Sherpa holds the company's thesis in a single word. A sherpa is a guide — experienced, capable, and uninterested in claiming the summit for themselves. They know the mountain because they've been up it before. They carry the load so you can focus on the climb.

The name encodes the relationship. We're not gurus who pronounce truth from a stage. We're practitioners who walk alongside you, carrying governance tools we built for our own work.

---

## Two Things, One Company

### The Framework

An open-source toolkit for running Human+AI collaborative workflows. Behavioral agent definitions, filesystem-based governance, and AI-native process conventions. The framework travels with your code — governance isn't a separate layer, it's built in.

### The Consulting

AI literacy workshops, agentic workforce consulting, and governance implementation. Every engagement runs on the same framework we ship as open source. We use the tools we sell.

### The Loop

The framework gets better because we use it on every engagement. The consulting gets better because we keep improving the framework. This isn't a tagline — it's the business model.

## Who It's For
<!-- Dunford: Best-Fit Customers -->

Three archetypes:

- **The Technical Leader** — Engineering managers and architects evaluating agentic workflows. They need governance that doesn't slow shipping. They'll try the framework first, then bring us in when the org needs help.
- **The Honest Executive** — C-suite leaders who know AI adoption is messy and want a partner who won't oversell. They value candor over credentials. They're tired of consultancies that pitch transformation without substance.
- **The Practitioner** — Developers and AI engineers who want better tooling for agent workflows. They'll use the framework independently and may never need consulting. They're our community, not our customers — and that's fine.

Common thread: people who value competence over performance.

## What Sherpa Is Not
<!-- Dunford: Competitive Alternatives (by negation) -->

- **Not a platform.** We're a framework and a consulting practice, not a SaaS product with a monthly fee.
- **Not an AI company.** We don't build AI models. We help teams govern the ones they use.
- **Not transformation theater.** We don't sell roadmaps and leave. We ship alongside you with the same tools.
- **Not guru-driven.** No thought leader personality cult. The framework is the authority, not any individual.

---

## What Makes Sherpa Different
<!-- Dunford: Unique Attributes + Value -->

- **We use what we ship.** Every consulting engagement runs on the same open-source framework. Not a demo, not a simplified version — the same tools.
- **Behavioral constraints, not identity prompts.** Agent roles are defined through what they *do*, not who they *are*. This is research-validated and structurally different from how most teams define AI agents.
- **Governance travels with the code.** Initiative lifecycle, quality gates, and behavioral constraints live in the filesystem alongside the code. No separate governance layer to maintain.
- **Anti-hype positioning.** We lead with real data (91% faking, 95% pilot failure) and honest guidance, not breathless promises about AI transformation.
