# Voice & Tone

How we write — the words, rhythm, and attitude that make copy feel like *us*.

---

## Voice (Consistent Always)

Voice stays constant across all contexts. This is who we are.

### Calm, Not Cold

Warm but not effusive. We're the colleague who explains things clearly, not the one who's over-excited about AI.

- Yes: "Welcome back."
- No: "We're SO excited to see you again!"

### Knowledgeable, Not Academic

We know our subject but don't lecture. Share understanding, not credentials.

- Yes: "Behavioral constraints keep agents predictable."
- No: "As recent research has demonstrated, behavioral constraint paradigms..."

### Present, Not Precious

Direct language. Short sentences when possible. No verbal decoration.

- Yes: "Your workflow shipped."
- No: "Your carefully crafted workflow has been successfully deployed."

### Honest, Not Cynical

We call out real problems without performing skepticism. Anti-hype, not anti-AI.

- Yes: "Most AI pilots fail because governance wasn't part of the plan."
- No: "AI is mostly hype and everyone's faking it."

## Tone (Shifts by Context)

Tone adapts to the situation while voice remains constant.

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Marketing / website | Warm, confident | "Ship AI workflows you can actually trust." |
| Framework docs | Clear, practical | "Define behavioral constraints in the agent role file." |
| Consulting content | Thoughtful, grounded | "The gap isn't talent. It's that governance comes last — if it comes at all." |
| Blog / thought leadership | Conversational, specific | "We ran this framework on 12 engagements before open-sourcing it." |
| Empty states | Inviting, unhurried | "Nothing here yet. Start with a proposal." |
| Errors | Honest, calm | "Something went wrong loading the initiative." |
| Success messages | Warm, brief | "Saved." or "You're in." |

## Word Lists

### Words We Use

*Notice, pattern, practice, approach, constraint, governance, workflow, guide, ship, trust, iterate, ground, evidence.*

These words are concrete, observational, and action-oriented.

### Words We Avoid

| Word/Phrase | Problem | Alternative |
|-------------|---------|-------------|
| Revolutionize | Hyperbolic | *improve*, *change*, *rethink* |
| Cutting-edge / bleeding-edge | Cliché tech marketing | Just describe what it does |
| AI-powered | Meaningless modifier | Be specific about what the AI does |
| Synergy | Corporate dead language | Name the actual benefit |
| Unlock | Marketing speak | *discover*, *explore*, *enable* |
| Supercharge | Hyperbolic | *improve*, *accelerate* |
| Game-changer | Empty superlative | Describe the actual change |
| Thought leader | Self-appointed title | Show the thinking, skip the label |
| Best-in-class | Unverifiable claim | Be specific about what's good |
| Leverage (verb) | Corporate jargon | *use*, *apply*, *build on* |
| Disrupt | Exhausted | Say what you actually do differently |
| Scalable solution | Vague | Be specific about what scales and how |
| Empower | Condescending | *enable*, *support*, or just describe the capability |
| Transform / Transformation | Overused in AI consulting | *change*, *improve*, *adopt*, or describe what actually changes |

## The Headline Test

If a headline could appear in a generic AI consulting pitch deck, rewrite it.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "Unlock the Power of AI Transformation" | "Ship AI workflows with governance built in" |
| "Revolutionizing Enterprise AI Adoption" | "Helping teams adopt AI without the chaos" |
| "Your AI Transformation Partner" | "Honest guidance for teams shipping AI" |
| "Supercharge Your AI Strategy" | "A framework and a team that knows how to use it" |

## Sentence Patterns

### Prefer

- Active voice: "The framework defines behavioral constraints."
- Direct statements: "This is your third initiative this quarter."
- You as subject: "You ship with governance built in."
- Specificity: "91% of C-suite execs admit faking AI knowledge."
- Plain language: "We use the same tools we sell."

### Avoid

- Passive voice: "It has been observed that AI governance is..."
- Hedging preambles: "It's important to note that..."
- Superlatives without evidence: "The best AI governance framework..."
- Identity claims: "We are the leading AI consultancy..."
- Tech jargon as decoration: "Leveraging cutting-edge AI to synergize..."

## Consulting Communication Guidelines

Consulting is core to our business. We communicate it matter-of-factly, not defensively.

### Frame Expertise as Practice, Not Authority

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "We've run this framework on every engagement" | "We are industry-leading experts" |
| "Here's what we've seen work" | "Our proprietary methodology guarantees..." |
| "We built the tool we use" | "Trust our world-class team" |

### Be Specific About What We Do

Vague consulting claims feel like every other consultancy. Name the practices.

- **Yes:** "We define behavioral constraints for each agent role, run governed workflows, and ship with quality guarantees."
- **No:** "We help organizations transform their AI strategy." (too vague)

### Avoid Defensive Language

Don't position against competitors. Don't apologize for being small.

| Defensive (avoid) | Confident (use) |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| "Unlike other consultancies, we..." | "We..." |
| "Despite being a smaller firm..." | (just state what you do) |
| "Rest assured that..." | (just state the fact) |
