# Messaging Framework

Three layers — positioning, narrative, per-persona messaging — that translate product-positioning into repeatable copy across channels.

---

## Layer 1: Positioning (Dunford)

### Competitive Alternatives

| Alternative | What it looks like |
|-------------|-------------------|
| No governance | Ship and hope. Agents hallucinate, overspend, ship unreviewed code. |
| DIY governance | Internal conventions and ad-hoc review. Works until the team scales or someone leaves. |
| Traditional consultancy | Proprietary methodology, slide deck deliverable, vendor dependency. |
| Platform-based tools | SaaS governance product (LatticeFlow, etc.) without consulting support. |

### Unique Attributes

- **Behavioral constraints, not identity prompts.** Research-validated. Structurally different from how most teams define agent roles.
- **Filesystem-based governance.** Travels with the code — no separate governance layer to maintain.
- **We use what we ship.** Same open-source framework for consulting engagements and community use.
- **Research-validated approach.** Grounded in published evidence, not marketing claims.

### Value by Persona

| Persona | Primary value |
|---------|--------------|
| Technical Leader | Governance that doesn't slow shipping. Framework they keep after we leave. |
| Honest Executive | Honest assessment of AI readiness. A partner who won't oversell. |
| Practitioner | Better tooling for agent workflows. Community, not a sales funnel. |

### Best-Fit Customers

People who value competence over performance. Teams that want to ship AI workflows — not talk about shipping them.

### Market Category

Behavioral governance for agentic workflows.

## Layer 2: Strategic Narrative (Raskin)

**The Shift.** AI agents are becoming autonomous workers, not just tools. The transition from AI-as-copilot to AI-as-workforce is underway.

**The Stakes.** Organizations that don't govern agentic workflows will ship unreliable, unauditable AI. The 95% pilot failure rate is a governance problem, not a technology problem.

**The Promised Land.** Teams shipping AI workflows with behavioral governance built in — predictable agents, auditable decisions, governance that travels with the code.

**The Magic Gifts.**
1. *The framework* — behavioral constraints, initiative lifecycle, quality gates. Open-source, filesystem-based, stays when we leave.
2. *The consulting* — guides who built the framework and use it daily. Practice, not theory.

**The Evidence.** Sherpa governs itself. Its own initiative system, task dispatch, and quality gates are the proof. The framework isn't a demo — it's the production system.

## Layer 3: Per-Persona Messaging

### Practitioner

**Primary message:** Better tooling for agentic workflows — open-source, behavioral, built by practitioners.
- Behavioral constraints keep agents predictable without slowing you down.
- Governance lives in the filesystem alongside your code. No separate layer.
- Built by people who ship with it daily.
- **Proof:** The framework governs its own development. Every convention is battle-tested.
- **CTA:** Try the framework. Use it independently. Join the community if it's useful.

### Technical Leader

**Primary message:** Governance that ships with the code — your team keeps it after we leave.
- Evaluate the open-source framework first. Bring us in when the org needs help.
- No vendor lock-in. The deliverable is a working governance system in your codebase, not a slide deck.
- Research-validated behavioral constraints outperform identity-based agent definitions.
- **Proof:** Every consulting engagement runs on the same framework we open-source.
- **CTA:** Start with the framework. Talk to us when you're ready to scale governance across teams.

### Honest Executive

**Primary message:** Honest guidance for AI adoption — grounded in evidence, not hype.
- 91% of C-suite execs admit faking AI knowledge. We start from reality.
- The 95% pilot failure rate is a governance problem. We address it structurally.
- We use the tools we sell. No proprietary black box.
- **Proof:** We built the governance framework for our own work, then open-sourced it.
- **CTA:** Start with a conversation. We'll tell you what we've seen work — and what hasn't.
