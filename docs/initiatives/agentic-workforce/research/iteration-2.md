# Iteration 2 — 2026-03-12

## Research Vectors

### Vector 1: Solo & Micro-Team AI Agency Landscape
**Question:** Who is actually running a solo or micro-team consulting/agency business powered by AI agents in 2025-2026?
**Full report:** [iteration-2/vector-1-solo-ai-agency-landscape.md](iteration-2/vector-1-solo-ai-agency-landscape.md)

**Key discoveries:**
- **Named practitioners found**: Joao Pedro Silva Setas (8 AI agent departments, GitHub Copilot + MCP), Daniel Bilsborough (Claude Code advisory, $5K+/session), Nick Saraev (AI automation educator, targeting $2M/yr), Jason Lemkin (replaced 10 sales staff with 20 AI agents)
- **The winning architecture is markdown agent files + simple dispatch + human review** — every successful practitioner converged here, validating Sherpa's approach
- **No one is running structured governance for agent consulting** — the closest examples are ad hoc (Telegram bot + folders, daily standup agents). Sherpa's initiative lifecycle and behavioral constraints are genuinely novel.
- **Content production is the proven entry point** — $25K-$40K/month at 75-85% margins with 8-12 clients using a three-model pipeline (Claude/GPT/Gemini)
- **"Vibe consulting" is emerging as a category** — 44 vibe coding agencies listed, J.P. Morgan documenting economics, shift from hourly to outcome-based pricing

**Implications:**
- Market is real but immature — no breakout success story at scale yet
- Sherpa's governance framework is differentiated; nobody else has it
- Content production and AI advisory are the two proven revenue models
- Teaching/meta-layer (frameworks, courses) may be more lucrative than running agencies
- Agent framework churn is a risk signal (Morningside AI domain for sale, Gartner 40% cancellation prediction)

## Synthesis

This iteration mapped the **ground truth of who is actually doing this** vs. the hype. The landscape reveals:

1. **Two viable archetypes**: High-ticket advisory (Bilsborough: $5K+/session, human expertise amplified by AI) and productized automation services (content agencies: $1.5K-$5K/month retainers, AI handles production).

2. **The governance gap is Sherpa's opportunity.** Every practitioner is building ad hoc systems. Nobody has behavioral agent definitions, initiative lifecycle, quality gates, or observable pipelines for agent-powered consulting. This is the strategic opening.

3. **The market is larger than visible.** Business Insider is covering unnamed founders. Reddit threads are looking for Claude Code freelancers. The practitioners exist but are not yet writing publicly about their systems. The first person to document a rigorous framework for this wins the category definition.
