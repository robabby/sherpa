# Vector 1: Deloitte & Accenture AI Transformation

**Question:** What are Deloitte and Accenture doing with AI internally in 2025-2026? How is it transforming their own operations?
**Agent dispatched:** 2026-03-15

## Findings

### Deloitte

**Internal AI Platforms:**
- **Zora AI** — Agentic AI platform unveiled at Nvidia GTC 2025. Built on full NVIDIA stack. Agents covering finance, HR, supply chain, procurement, sales/marketing, customer service. Targeting 25% cost reduction and 40% productivity increase internally. Plans to deploy for thousands of internal users by end of 2025. Available to clients as cloud subscription.
- **Omnia** — Global cloud-based audit platform with GenAI/agentic capabilities (July 2025). AI performs initial audit doc reviews, drafts accounting memos, extracts data, detects patterns. Available to ~85,000 Audit & Assurance professionals globally.
- **Global Agentic Network** — Launched May 2025. Connected ecosystem of AI agent offerings across delivery centers in APAC, EMEA, North America. No agent count published (unlike McKinsey's 20K).
- **Enterprise AI Navigator** — Toolkit for AI planning: opportunity scanner, impact evaluator, process redesign tool, agent creation hub. Claims to halve AI planning time.

**Workforce Changes:**
- UK: up to 180 advisory roles cut in 2025, following 250 at-risk Oct 2024 and 800 in Sept 2024.
- **Job titles being scrapped** effective June 1, 2026. Structural reshape, not mass layoffs.
- No headline layoff number comparable to Accenture's 11K or McKinsey's 5K.

**Investment & Training:**
- **$3 billion** committed to gen AI through FY2030.
- **Deloitte AI Academy**: 120,000+ in pipeline, 58,000 trained, 40% GenAI fluency across firm.
- **Anthropic certification partnership**: 15,000 practitioners targeted.

### Accenture

**Internal AI Platforms:**
- **AATA (Accenture Advanced Technology Agent)** — Agentic AI integration platform, won 2025 CIO 100 Award. 100+ active agents. VPN config time reduced from 30 minutes to 2 minutes (93% reduction). Orchestration layer above existing RPA/CI/CD.
- **AI Refinery + Distiller Framework** — Enterprise platform for building/deploying agents (June 2025). Insights from ~2,000 internal developers. Includes agent memory management, multi-agent collaboration, governance/observability. 12 industry agent solutions at launch, targeting 100+.

**Workforce Changes:**
- **11,000 layoffs in September 2025** ($865M "business optimization"). Sweet: "exiting people where reskilling is not a viable path."
- Total ~22,000 headcount reduction in 2025 through voluntary exits and targeted layoffs.
- Already rehiring: 4,000 jobs in Q1 FY2026, ending at 783,691 workers.
- **AI talent: 40,000 (2023) → 77,000 (2025)**, nearing 80K target.

**AI Adoption Mandate:**
- **550,000 employees trained in GenAI** (70% of workforce) in one year.
- **Promotions now require demonstrated AI tool usage.** Weekly AI tool log-ins monitored, Copilot session data tracked, fed into dashboards visible to practice leads and HR.
- Regional exemptions for 12 European countries (GDPR/labor law) and US federal workers.

**Revenue:**
- GenAI bookings: $3B (FY2024) → $5.1B (FY2025), nearly doubled.
- GenAI revenues tripled to $2.7B.
- Record new bookings $20.9B total, GenAI contributing $2.2B in most recent quarter.

## Implications

- Deloitte's approach is more structural (title elimination, platform building) vs. Accenture's mandate-driven (surveillance dashboards, promotion gates). Both create governance gaps.
- "Who governs the governors?" is wide open — neither firm publicly addresses how they govern their own internal AI agent deployments with independent oversight.
- The training-to-adoption gap (Accenture had to monitor logins after training 550K) is a service opportunity. Gap between "trained" and "using effectively with governance" is where value lives.
- Agent orchestration at scale (100+ at Accenture, uncounted at Deloitte) needs behavioral constraints, lifecycle management, and audit trails — exactly Sherpa's system.
