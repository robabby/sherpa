# Vector 3: Client Defection & DIY Trends

**Question:** Are enterprise clients defecting from traditional consulting firms in 2025-2026? What does the shift to DIY AI capabilities look like?
**Agent dispatched:** 2026-03-15

## Findings

### HFS/IBM Survey: 65% Say Traditional Consulting Fails

- **65% of enterprise executives** say traditional consulting models "no longer deliver real value"; **83% say AI-powered consulting delivers more**. Global survey of 1,002 senior executives across 16 industries and 14 countries (November 2025).
- **Headcount-based contracts collapsing**: 49% of contracts today are tied to staff numbers, but only 16% expect this model within two years.
- **Fewer than 30%** of enterprises fully prepared across workforce, data, governance, vendor management. Only **20% have governance structures**, just **14% use AI-specific contracts**.

### Enterprise AI Spending Bypassing Consultants

- **Enterprise gen AI software spend: $37B in 2025** (up from $11.5B in 2024, $1.7B in 2023 — 3.2x YoY). Application layer alone: $19B.
- **Departmental AI spending: $7.3B** in 2025, up 4.1x YoY. Coding dominates at $4.0B (55%). This is direct tool spend, not consulting fees.
- VCs predict enterprises will spend more on AI in 2026 but through **fewer vendors** — consolidation, not expansion of consulting relationships.

### Utilization Rates Falling — Hard Evidence

- **Resources Connection (RGP)**: Consulting segment revenue **dropped 28.8%** in Q2 FY2026 due to **33.8% decrease in billable hours**. Cause: "reduced demand in traditional finance roles as clients increasingly adopt AI and automation."
- Industry-wide firm billable utilization trending below 70% and falling.

### Pyramid to Obelisk Structural Collapse

- **HBR (September 2025)** documented shift from pyramid to "obelisk" (narrow, senior-heavy, AI-augmented). Three surviving roles: AI facilitators (early-career), engagement architects (experienced), client leaders (relationships).
- **Unity Advisory** launched by former Big Four partners with $300M backing — pods of senior consultants + proprietary AI. No large entry-level cohorts.
- **Gartner**: 40% of consulting tasks are automatable.

### Sector-Specific Patterns

- **Financial services**: Fastest to automate away consulting roles — heavy quant infrastructure makes AI adoption natural.
- **Technology**: Building internal capabilities fastest — replacing "multimillion-dollar consulting fees" with internal AI teams.
- **Healthcare**: Accountability reckoning — Zimmer Biomet sued Deloitte for $172M over failed SAP implementation.
- **Energy transition, cybersecurity, sovereign cloud**: Growth areas but demand is for hyper-specialized niche firms, not generalists.

### AI CoE Trend

- Shift from project-based to program-based AI consulting complete by mid-2025. Successful consultants design multi-year transformation programs with capability-building.
- **Deloitte State of AI 2026**: Only 21% of organizations have proper governance for autonomous AI agents, despite 75% planning deployment within two years.

### Market Growing but Shifting

- Overall consulting market not shrinking yet — Deloitte $70.5B (+4.8%), EY $53.2B (+4%), KPMG (+5.1%). But growth far below pandemic-era double digits.
- **Execution-centric firms growing 2x faster** than strategy-pure firms. Clients want implementation, not slide decks.
- **AI governance platform market: $492M in 2026**, expected to surpass $1B by 2030 (Gartner).
- Digital transformation consulting expanding at 28.5% CAGR. Strategy consulting at 9.9%. Everything else sluggish.

## Implications

- The governance vacuum is quantified: 75% plan autonomous agents, only 21% have governance. This is Sherpa's exact market.
- Headcount billing dying (49% → 16% in two years) means a firm that never adopted it has no legacy to shed.
- The "help them build it internally" model wins — enterprises want capability transfer, not dependency.
- AI governance platform market ($492M → $1B) is large enough for specialists, small enough that Big Four aren't dominating.
- Niche beats generalist — boutiques gaining traction in AI governance specifically.

## Open Questions

1. How fast is the AI CoE internalization cycle? If 12-18 months, what does recurring revenue look like?
2. Is the 65% dissatisfaction about quality or pricing? Boutique rates with senior delivery might score differently.
3. Which specific governance frameworks are enterprises adopting? Standards still fragmented.
4. Are consulting layoffs cyclical (post-pandemic) or structural (automation)? RGP data looks structural.
