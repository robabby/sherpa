# Vector 2: BCG, Bain & KPMG Strategic Response

**Question:** How are the strategy-pure firms and KPMG responding to AI disruption? How does their strategy differ from the Big Four?
**Agent dispatched:** 2026-03-15

## Findings

### BCG

**AI Partnerships — Hedging Across Providers:**
- Partnered with **Anthropic** (Claude access for clients), joined **OpenAI Frontier Alliances** (Feb 2026) alongside McKinsey, Accenture, Capgemini.
- Nine total tech partnerships: Anthropic, AWS, Google, IBM, Microsoft, OpenAI, Salesforce, SAP.

**Internal Tools:**
- **GENE** (Sept 2024): GPT-4o-based agent ingesting hundreds of pages of proprietary research for consultant use.
- **Deckster**: AI slide-generation tool with API extending to client slide libraries.
- **BCG X**: Expanded to 3,000+ engineers. Building a **multi-agent mesh** with plans for 50+ reusable agent blueprints by mid-2026.

**Workforce:**
- Grew headcount to ~34,000, publicly defying layoff trend.
- But paused experienced-hire recruiting, narrowed school targeting to top-tier/Ivy.
- Reports of "quiet layoffs" — high performers let go before bonuses while maintaining growth narrative.
- Frozen starting salaries for three consecutive years (alongside McKinsey and Bain).

### Bain

**OpenAI-Exclusive Bet (Broadening):**
- Closest OpenAI consulting partner since 2022. **OpenAI Center of Excellence** with ~50 dedicated employees.
- March 2026: formalized partnerships with **seven flagship VC firms** to drive AI innovation for clients.

**Revenue:**
- AI and technology now **30% of total revenue**, projected to reach **50%**.
- Marquee client: Coca-Cola (GPT-4 + DALL-E for marketing/ops).

**Internal Tools:**
- AI Sales Representative Copilot (pharma/medtech): real-time coaching, customer insights, automated follow-up.
- Regulatory Drafting Automation: agentic AI generating first-draft regulatory submissions with auditability.
- Employees created **2,000+ custom GPTs** internally.
- **60% adoption of ChatGPT Enterprise** within two weeks of rollout.

**Team Structure Transformation:**
- Shifting from teams of **40 specialists** to **3-4 person "full-stack" problem solver** groups.
- New roles: engagement leads, customer lifecycle leads, data science leads, compliance leads.
- AI/data/analytics/engineering practice: **1,500+ experts**.

**Hiring:**
- Shifted to **just-in-time hiring** — recruiting Associate Consultants as late as January 2026.
- Fired **150+ consultants** in India offices.
- Frozen starting salaries for third consecutive year.

### KPMG — The Overlooked AI Play

**Most Structurally Defensible Position:**
- **KPMG Clara**: Global smart audit platform rebuilt on Azure AI Foundry. AI agents now handle substantive audit procedures — expense vouching, search for unrecorded liabilities, accrued expenses analysis. More agents planned for controls testing and financial statement analysis.
- **AI Assurance services** (Sept 2025): Model risk assessments, control testing, quantitative assessments, model validation, real-time systems assessments.
- **$4.2 billion** committed to AI-backed technology investments.
- Coined shift from "year of the agent" (2025) to **"age of the agent orchestrator"** (2026) — focusing on governance, monitoring, security, integration at scale.

**Why KPMG Matters:** They're building AI governance tools *for the thing they already audit*. When enterprises need agent validation and compliance, KPMG has both the audit mandate and the AI tooling. Natural moat that strategy-pure firms cannot replicate.

### Strategy vs. Big Four Vulnerability

| Factor | MBB (Strategy-Pure) | Big Four |
|--------|---------------------|----------|
| Revenue base | 100% advisory fees | Audit + tax + advisory (recurring, regulated) |
| AI exposure | Core product (analysis, insight) directly automatable | Audit/tax automatable but regulation creates moats |
| AI investment | BCG X 3K engineers; Bain 1.5K AI experts | Deloitte $3B; KPMG $4.2B; EY $1B US |
| Pricing shift | ~25% outcome-based (McKinsey); moving slowly | EY pushing "service-as-a-software" |

**Strategy consulting is more vulnerable short-term:**
- Pure strategy work (analysis, positioning, scenario modeling) is where AI is strongest.
- No regulatory moat — unlike audit (requires human sign-off by law).
- Pyramid more exposed: MBB junior cohorts do exactly the work AI automates fastest.
- Specialists now command **30-40% fee premiums** over generalists (Q3 2025).

### Pricing Model Disruption

- McKinsey: ~25% outcome-based, up from near-zero.
- EY: Pushing "service-as-a-software" model.
- 73% of consulting clients prefer pricing tied to measurable outcomes.
- AI consulting rates 2026: $600-$1,200/day independent; value-based (10-40% of measurable outcomes) gaining traction.

## Implications

- KPMG's "age of the agent orchestrator" framing is the closest any incumbent has come to Sherpa's thesis — but they're building for audit compliance, not development governance.
- Bain's 40→4 team compression validates small-team consulting as structurally native, not a compromise.
- BCG hedging across 9 AI providers means provider-agnostic governance (Sherpa's position) is the right bet.
- The strategy-pure firms' higher vulnerability creates content opportunity: "the work AI automates fastest is the work MBB charges the most for."

## Open Questions

1. How sticky are AI provider partnerships? BCG hedged across 9; Bain bet on OpenAI. Provider-agnostic governance advantage?
2. Will the obelisk model kill the talent pipeline? Where do future senior consultants come from without apprenticeship?
3. How will audit regulators respond to AI agents in KPMG Clara? Standards bodies haven't ruled on AI-generated audit evidence.
4. Is Bain's 30→50% AI revenue trajectory sustainable, or does it commoditize them against Accenture?
5. What specific AI governance frameworks are clients buying? Understanding concrete deliverables clarifies where convention-based (Sherpa) vs. compliance-based (Big Four) approaches fit.
