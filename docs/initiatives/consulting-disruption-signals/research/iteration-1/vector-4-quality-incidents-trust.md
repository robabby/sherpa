# Vector 4: Quality Incidents & Trust Erosion

**Question:** What specific AI quality failures and trust incidents have occurred at major consulting firms in 2025-2026?
**Agent dispatched:** 2026-03-15

## Findings

### Specific AI Quality Failures

**Deloitte Australia — Welfare Penalties Report (Oct 2025)**
- 237-page, AU$440,000 report for Dept of Employment. Law professor catalogued ~20 errors: fabricated academic references, non-existent footnotes, fabricated quote attributed to a Federal Court judge.
- Revised report disclosed Azure OpenAI (GPT-4o) usage — not originally disclosed.
- Deloitte refunded only AU$97,587 (less than a quarter). Senate estimates hearings ensued.

**Deloitte Canada — Newfoundland Healthcare Report (Nov 2025)**
- 526-page, C$1.6M healthcare report. Contained false citations from fabricated academic papers. Cited real researchers on papers they never worked on.
- Deloitte claimed "AI was not used to write the report; it was selectively used to support a small number of research citations" — but pattern matched classic LLM hallucination.

**UK FRC: All Big Six Failing on AI Audit Quality (June 2025)**
- Financial Reporting Council reviewed all six major firms: "no formal monitoring performed to quantify the audit quality impact" of AI/automated tools.
- All but one firm had zero KPIs for AI tools in audit. Published first-ever AI-in-audit guidance.

**KPMG Global Survey: 57% of Workers Admit AI Errors (April 2025)**
- 48,000+ respondents, 47 countries. 57% admitted workplace mistakes due to AI errors.
- 64% admitted putting less effort knowing they can lean on AI. 58% rely on AI output without assessing it. 53% present AI content as their own.
- 46% uploaded sensitive data to public AI platforms. ~50% use AI without knowing if allowed.

### Insurance Industry Response

**Verisk AI Exclusion Endorsements (Jan 1, 2026)**
- New general liability exclusion forms: CG 40 47 01 26 (excludes BI, property damage, and personal injury from gen AI) and CG 40 48 01 26 (Coverage B only).
- Strong carrier interest reported. Carriers can optionally adopt.

**"Silent AI" Coverage Crisis**
- Before 2025, most E&O policies were silent on AI. By early 2026, silence is disappearing from renewals.
- Many professional liability policies limit coverage to services "provided by natural persons."
- "Swiss cheese effect": multiple policies with holes leaving gaps where exclusions align.

**Testudo: First Dedicated AI Liability Insurer**
- Lloyd's Lab-backed, launched late 2025. Claims-made policies covering AI-generated errors, IP infringement, regulatory investigations.
- Expanded capacity to $9.25M per insured (March 2026) with Atrium and QBE support.
- Gen-AI litigation increased **137% year-over-year** per Testudo data.

### Regulatory Actions

- **Australian Senate**: Deloitte incident became focal point for consulting firm integrity inquiry.
- **UK FRC**: First-ever AI-in-audit guidance (June 2025) alongside finding all six firms lacked quality monitoring.
- **US GSA**: GSAR 552.239-7001 (March 2026) — contractors must disclose all AI systems used in contract performance within 30 days. Applies to all AI used to perform contracts, not just AI sold to government.
- **OMB M-25-22**: Bars vendors from using non-public government data to train public AI. AI-specific solicitation provisions from October 2025.

### Client Contract Changes

- ABA Formal Opinion 512 (2024): Boilerplate AI disclosure insufficient. Must be specific, transparent, opt-in consent, granular client control.
- Illinois HB 3773 (Jan 1, 2026): Notification required when AI assists with hiring, performance, promotions, discipline.
- Modern consulting agreements expected to include explicit AI disclosure clauses, human-review guarantees, data handling commitments.

### Trust Metrics

- **Edelman 2025-2026**: AI trust in US: 32%. UK: 36%. Germany: 39%.
- **HBR Analytic Services (Dec 2025)**: Only **6% of companies fully trust AI agents** for core business processes. Only 12% say governance controls are ready for agentic AI.

### Professional Standards

- **ISACA**: Advanced in AI Audit (AAIA) certification launched 2025. ITAF 5th edition (March 2026) incorporating AI/ML auditing.
- **AICPA**: "Closing the AI Trust Gap: The Role of the CPA in AI Assurance" — governance, risk management, baseline CPA practices for AI.

## Implications

- Deloitte's two-incident pattern is the canonical warning — same failure mode (LLM hallucinated citations, no disclosure, partial refund) across two countries in two months. Every prospect will have heard this story.
- The FRC finding (zero AI quality KPIs across all six firms) is the most damning systemic data point. It proves the governance gap isn't anecdotal.
- Insurance is becoming a forcing function — E&O policies actively excluding AI coverage creates a concrete selling point for firms with documented quality-gate processes.
- Only 6% trust AI agents (HBR) + only 12% have governance ready = the market is 88% unserved.
- ISACA AAIA and AICPA AI assurance frameworks create credential opportunities that pair with governance tooling.
