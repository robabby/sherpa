# Vector 5: Revenue Model & Pricing Disruption

**Question:** How are consulting firm revenue and pricing models changing in 2025-2026 due to AI?
**Agent dispatched:** 2026-03-15

## Findings

### The Billable Hours Paradox

- McKinsey's Lilli saves ~1.5 million hours in 2025 (30% reduction in search/synthesis). But only ~25% of fees are outcome-based — 75% remain traditional billing.
- Efficiency gains currently pocketed as margin, not passed to clients. 10-week projects executed in 6 weeks, cost base falls 30-40%, pricing stays anchored to human time.
- Creates a **micro-arbitrage window** that closes as clients get smarter.

### Outcome-Based Pricing — Growing But Minority

- McKinsey: 25% of global fees now outcome-linked (up from near-zero five years ago).
- 73% of consulting clients *prefer* outcome-based, but adoption lags demand.
- Gartner: 40% of enterprise SaaS to include outcome-based elements by 2026 (up from 15%). Consulting follows this trend.
- Value-based pricing accelerating because time-based anchor becomes absurd with AI leverage.

### Revenue Per Consultant — Rising (For Now)

- Partners can now handle **2x as many projects** with AI leverage.
- Hybrid teams (human + AI) deliver projects **35% faster** with similar quality.
- Top firms average **$430K revenue per consultant** annually. AI pushing this higher.
- Only holds while clients don't demand savings. Client awareness rising fast.

### Big Six AI Revenue

- **Accenture**: $3.6B AI bookings FY2025, $1.1B in Q1 FY2026 (120% YoY).
- **BCG**: 20% of $13.5B revenue ($2.7B) from AI advisory — didn't exist two years prior.
- **McKinsey**: Expects 40% of business to be AI-related.
- **Deloitte**: $3B committed to gen AI through 2030.
- Collectively Big Four + MBB: **$10B+ into AI** since 2023.

### AI-Specific Service Lines — Pricing

- AI strategy consulting: $200-$500+/hour US; $600-$1,200/day senior.
- Project-based: Small pilots $10K-$40K; medium $40K-$150K; enterprise $150K-$1M+.
- **Specialization premium: 30-40%** over generalists.
- Value-based AI fees: 10-40% of measured cost savings or revenue increases.
- **EU AI Act compliance market**: AI compliance monitoring alone forecast to reach $4.1B by 2030.

### AI-Native Competitor Pricing

- **Palantir**: Subscription + usage expansion ("land and expand"). No per-seat. US commercial revenue surged 121% YoY in Q3 2025.
- **C3.ai**: Productized AI applications as alternative to custom consulting.
- Legacy SaaS firms failing to pivot from seat-based to consumption-based seeing valuation contraction.

### Market Size — Still Growing

- Global management consulting: ~$466-491B in 2024-2025, growing 5-6% CAGR.
- **AI consulting specifically**: $11-22B in 2025, growing at **26-36% CAGR** to $91-258B by 2033-2035.
- Technology consulting to surpass $400B in 2026.
- Corporate AI spending expected to **double in 2026** (0.8% → 1.7% of revenues).

### Pricing Model Shift

- Per-seat dying in SaaS and consulting. Legacy firms clinging to it see valuation pressure.
- **Hybrid model emerging as consensus**: base retainer (predictability) + outcome components (upside).
- Retainer tiers in AI consulting: Essential $2K-$5K/month, Standard $5K-$15K/month, Comprehensive $15K-$50K/month.
- EY floating "subscription consulting" — continuous AI monitoring/optimization for recurring fee.

### Profit Margins

- AI as **synthetic labor** — replicating pyramid base without salaries. Margin expansion for firms keeping gains in-house.
- Specialized AI consultants achieving **40%+ profit margins**.
- Value-based pricing increases project revenue **40%+ vs. hourly billing**.
- Strategy work now **less than 20% of McKinsey's business** — down from historical core.

## Implications

- Never anchor to time. Every AI efficiency gain cannibalizes time-based revenue. Price on value from day one.
- Hybrid model (base retainer + outcome upside) is the sweet spot for a new firm. Recurring revenue + value capture.
- Specialize ruthlessly — 30-40% premium for specialization. Big Six can't match boutique depth in narrow domains.
- EU AI Act (Aug 2026) creates a near-term regulatory consulting goldmine. Small firms move faster.
- Revenue per person is the north star metric. AI leverage means a small firm can deliver what used to require 10 people.
- The micro-arbitrage window (charging old prices at new speeds) is temporary. Build for value-based from the start.
