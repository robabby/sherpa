# Iteration 1 — 2026-03-15

## Findings

### Vector 1: Deloitte & Accenture AI Transformation
**Question:** What are Deloitte and Accenture doing with AI internally?
**Full report:** [iteration-1/vector-1-deloitte-accenture-ai.md](iteration-1/vector-1-deloitte-accenture-ai.md)

- Deloitte: Zora AI (agentic platform), Omnia (AI audit for 85K professionals), Global Agentic Network, $3B committed through 2030, scrapping job titles June 2026
- Accenture: AATA (100+ agents), Distiller framework, 11K layoffs + 22K total exits in 2025, then rehired 4K. AI talent 40K→77K. **Promotions now require demonstrated AI tool usage** — Copilot login tracking feeds HR dashboards
- Neither firm publishes agent counts comparable to McKinsey's 20K. Neither has publicly addressed how they govern their own internal agents.

**Implications:** The "who governs the governors?" angle is wide open. Accenture's surveillance-as-governance (tracking logins for promotion) is an anti-pattern that behavioral constraints would replace.

### Vector 2: BCG, Bain & KPMG Strategic Response
**Question:** How are strategy-pure firms and KPMG responding differently?
**Full report:** [iteration-1/vector-2-bcg-bain-kpmg-strategy.md](iteration-1/vector-2-bcg-bain-kpmg-strategy.md)

- BCG: Hedging across 9 AI providers, BCG X at 3K engineers, building multi-agent mesh (50+ blueprints by mid-2026). Publicly growing headcount to ~34K while quietly pausing experienced-hire recruiting.
- Bain: Shifting from 40-person teams to 3-4 person "full-stack" pods. AI = 30% of revenue, targeting 50%. 2,000+ custom GPTs built internally. 60% ChatGPT Enterprise adoption in two weeks.
- KPMG: **Most structurally defensible position** — Clara audit platform with AI agents for substantive audit procedures. AI Assurance services. $4.2B committed. Coined "age of the agent orchestrator."
- All three MBB firms froze starting salaries for third consecutive year.

**Implications:** KPMG's "age of the agent orchestrator" is closest to Sherpa's thesis but serves audit compliance, not development governance. Bain's 40→4 team compression validates small-team consulting as structurally native.

### Vector 3: Client Defection & DIY Trends
**Question:** Are enterprise clients defecting from traditional consulting?
**Full report:** [iteration-1/vector-3-client-defection-diy-trends.md](iteration-1/vector-3-client-defection-diy-trends.md)

- HFS/IBM: 65% say traditional consulting fails. Headcount contracts collapsing (49%→16% in two years). Only 20% have governance structures, 14% use AI-specific contracts.

- HBR: Pyramid→Obelisk. Three surviving roles: AI facilitators, engagement architects, client leaders.
- **AI governance platform market: $492M in 2026, $1B+ by 2030** (Gartner).

**Implications:** The governance market is independently sized and growing. Enterprises want capability transfer (help them build it, then own it), not dependency. Execution-centric firms growing 2x faster than strategy-pure.

### Vector 4: Quality Incidents & Trust Erosion
**Question:** What specific AI quality failures have occurred at consulting firms?
**Full report:** [iteration-1/vector-4-quality-incidents-trust.md](iteration-1/vector-4-quality-incidents-trust.md)

- Deloitte: Two hallucination scandals in two months (Australia AU$440K report, Canada C$1.6M report). Same failure mode: fabricated citations, no AI disclosure, partial refund.
- UK FRC: All six major firms have zero KPIs for AI tools in audit. No formal quality monitoring.
- KPMG's own survey: 57% of workers admit AI-fueled errors. 53% present AI content as own work.
- **Insurance shifting:** Verisk AI exclusion endorsements effective Jan 1, 2026. "Silent AI" coverage disappearing from renewals. Testudo launched as first dedicated AI liability insurer.
- **Regulatory acceleration:** GSA requires AI disclosure in 30 days for federal contracts. Illinois HB 3773 effective Jan 2026. EU AI Act full applicability Aug 2026.
- **Trust floor:** Only 6% of companies fully trust AI agents (HBR). Only 12% say governance controls are ready. AI trust in US: 32% (Edelman).

**Implications:** Insurance is becoming a forcing function — firms with documented quality gates may retain coverage; firms without may become uninsurable. The Deloitte pattern is the canonical warning every prospect has heard.

### Vector 5: Revenue Model & Pricing Disruption
**Question:** How are engagement models changing across the industry?
**Full report:** [iteration-1/vector-5-revenue-model-pricing.md](iteration-1/vector-5-revenue-model-pricing.md)

- McKinsey: 25% outcome-based (up from near-zero). 73% of clients prefer outcomes but adoption lags.
- Micro-arbitrage window: firms charging 10-week prices for 6-week AI-assisted work. Window closing as clients get smarter.
- Revenue per consultant rising (partners handle 2x projects) — only holds while clients don't demand savings.
- AI consulting market: $11-22B in 2025, growing 26-36% CAGR to $91-258B by 2033-2035.
- Specialization premium: 30-40% over generalists. Value-based pricing increases revenue 40%+ vs. hourly.
- EU AI Act compliance monitoring market alone: $4.1B by 2030.
- Hybrid model emerging as consensus: base retainer + outcome-linked upside.

**Implications:** Never anchor to time. Build outcome-based from day one — no legacy to unwind. Specialize ruthlessly. Revenue per person is the north star metric.

## Synthesis

Five vectors, surveying all Big Six firms across workforce, tooling, clients, quality, and pricing, converge on a single structural insight:

**The consulting industry is experiencing a coordination failure.** Every firm is investing billions in AI capability ($10B+ collectively since 2023) while simultaneously lacking the governance infrastructure to deploy it safely. The evidence is damning:

- **20,000+ AI agents at McKinsey** with no public governance framework
- **Zero quality KPIs** for AI tools across all six major audit firms (FRC)
- **57% of workers** admitting AI-fueled errors (KPMG's own survey)
- **Only 6% of companies** trusting AI agents for core processes (HBR)
- **Two hallucination scandals at Deloitte** in two months, same failure mode

This is not a capability gap. It's a **governance gap**. And it creates three structural openings:

### Opening 1: The Governance Layer Market
Gartner sizes AI governance platforms at $492M (2026) → $1B+ (2030). EU AI Act compliance monitoring alone: $4.1B by 2030. The Big Six are building governance *features inside their platforms* (KPMG Clara, Deloitte's Trustworthy AI, Accenture's Distiller) — but nobody is building governance as a standalone, framework-level capability. Sherpa's behavioral agents + quality gates + lifecycle governance fills exactly this vacancy.

### Opening 2: The Pricing Model Vacuum
73% of clients want outcome-based pricing. Only 25% of McKinsey's fees are structured that way. The micro-arbitrage window (old prices, new speeds) is temporary. A firm that never adopts time-based billing has no legacy to shed. The hybrid model (retainer + outcome upside) is the consensus direction.

### Opening 3: The Trust Arbitrage
AI trust is at 32% (US, Edelman). Only 6% trust AI agents. Insurance is actively excluding AI coverage. But AI adoption is at 88%. The gap between adoption and trust is where governance lives. A firm that can demonstrate verifiable quality gates — behavioral constraints, documented audit trails, measurable outcomes — captures the trust premium that the Big Six are eroding.

**The meta-insight:** The Big Six are simultaneously the best proof that the governance market exists (they're deploying thousands of ungoverned agents) and the worst possible providers of governance solutions (they can't even govern their own agents, per the FRC review). This is Sherpa's positioning in one sentence: **"We have the governance layer the big firms are deploying without."**
