# Vector 3: Internalization Cycle Speed

**Question:** How fast do enterprises build internal AI governance after external consulting help? What does "done" look like?
**Agent dispatched:** 2026-03-15

## Findings

### Engagement Duration Benchmarks

- **Foundational setup** (governance policies, risk framework, initial controls): 3-6 months.
- **Enterprise-wide integration** (scaling across business units, embedding in workflows): 12-24 months.
- **Full AI implementation** (pilot to enterprise scale): 18-36 months for multiple use cases.
- **Average consulting client relationship**: 18-24 months, with 6-12 months building institutional knowledge before peak value.

**Adjacent domain comparisons:**
- SOX compliance: 18+ months to full readiness (6-12 months for smaller orgs).
- Data governance: 3-9 months project; 12-18 months full-scale maturity.
- Cybersecurity (MSSP to internal SOC): 6-12 months basic, 18-24 months comprehensive.

### AI CoE Standing-Up Timeline

| Phase | Duration | What Happens |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| 1. Foundation | 0-3 months | Executive sponsorship, scope, minimum viable governance team |
| 2. Early wins | 3-6 months | First use cases governed, credibility built. **60-90 day measurable outcomes required.** |
| 3. Scale | 6-18 months | Standards, tooling, repeatable processes. CoE running independently for day-to-day. |
| 4. Mature | 18-24+ months | CoE shifts from centralized control to advisory. Governance embedded in platform ops. |

- Organizations with structured CoEs reduce project delivery time by 40-60%.
- JP Morgan Chase's MLCOE: 2+ years of AI-powered LLM payment validation, 15-20% reduction in false positives.
- Google: 200+ FTEs dedicated to ethical AI; 32,000 employees engaged in AI Principles training since 2019.

### What Stays Outsourced After Internalization

**Companies never fully stop outsourcing.** Core governance goes internal; specific functions remain external:
- Regulatory monitoring and compliance advisory (regulations evolving continuously)
- Independent model auditing and bias testing (third-party credibility)
- Third-party risk management (vendor/partner AI assessment)
- Periodic governance maturity reassessment (external benchmarking)

**What goes internal:** day-to-day policy enforcement, model monitoring, documented decision-making, accountability structures, internal training.

### Maturity Models Converge on 4-6 Levels

- Credo AI: 6 levels (Exploring → Leading). Advancing one full level: 12-24 months.
- Reaching Level 2: 2-3 months. "Formalizing" stage: 4-6 months.
- **Gartner**: 80% of large orgs claim governance initiatives, fewer than half demonstrate measurable maturity. Orgs with governance platforms are **3.4x more likely to achieve high effectiveness**.
- Only 20% of enterprises have mature governance for autonomous AI agents specifically.

### Market Shifting to Ongoing Models

- **65% of Fortune 500** have retainer relationships with AI consultants (up from project-based).
- Retainer pricing: $5K-$25K/month for ongoing AI consulting access.
- "Land and expand" dominant: goal is 10% → 70% of client base on recurring contracts at $180-220/hour.
- **40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by end of 2027** due to costs, unclear value, or inadequate governance (Gartner).

### Retention Risk

- **68% of satisfied B2B clients churn within 18 months** (Harvard Business School).
- 10-percentage-point retention improvement (70%→80%) generates ~$4.2M incremental annual revenue for a $50M firm.
- Phase 3 sustain retainer needs visible, recurring deliverables — not just "available if you need us."

## The Taper Model

**"Done" is not a cliff — it's a taper:**

| Phase | Duration | Consulting Role | Pricing Model |
|-------|----------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Build** | Months 1-3 | Heavy — consultant leads | Fixed-scope, outcome-priced |
| **Operate** | Months 3-9 | Medium — consultant coaches | Declining retainer ($5K-15K/month) |
| **Advise** | Months 9-18 | Light — periodic reviews | Advisory retainer ($2K-5K/month) |
| **Sustain** | Months 18+ | Residual — audits, regulatory, benchmarking | Quarterly deliverable retainer |

## Implications

- **Design for the taper, not the handoff.** Three-phase model: Build (project) → Operate (coaching retainer) → Sustain (lightweight retainer). Each phase has different deliverables and pricing.
- **The framework IS the capability transfer mechanism.** Sherpa's behavioral agents, quality gates, and lifecycle management are what gets transferred. The consulting engagement is "install and teach Sherpa's governance system."
- **60-90 day credibility window is non-negotiable.** Phase 1 must produce tangible, operational governance artifacts within this window.
- **Maturity model as engagement scaffolding.** Each level maps to specific deliverables. Client sees where they are and what "done" looks like — solves "when do we not need you" transparently.
- **Sustain retainer must deliver visible recurring value.** Not "available if you need us." Concrete: quarterly governance scorecard, regulatory change assessment, annual independent audit.
- **68% churn at 18 months** means Phase 3 must be designed to survive a renewal decision.
