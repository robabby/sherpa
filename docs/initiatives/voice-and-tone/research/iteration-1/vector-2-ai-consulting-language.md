# Vector 2: AI Consulting Language Landscape

**Question:** How do AI consulting firms actually sound on their websites? What language patterns dominate? Where can Sherpa differentiate?
**Agent dispatched:** 2026-03-13

## Findings

### Firms Surveyed (13 total)

**Tier 1 — Global Giants:**
- **Accenture** — "Let There Be Change" / "Reinvention" obsession. "Together, we can reinvent anything." Aspirational-corporate, heavy on "unprecedented," "human ingenuity."
- **McKinsey/QuantumBlack** — "Intelligence. Beautifully Engineered." / "Hybrid Intelligence." Polished, intellectual, premium. Almost literary. The only large firm with aesthetic ambition in language.
- **BCG/BCG X** — "Scaling AI can create massive competitive advantage." / "BCG X disrupts the present." Aggressive-competitive, tech-startup energy.
- **Deloitte** — "Agentic AI is the operating logic of the future." Framework-heavy, buzzword-dense. Worst offender for compound noun inflation ("Enterprise AI Navigator," "Global Agentic Network").
- **Bain/Vector Digital** — Process-oriented, slightly more restrained. Still "competitive advantage" and "turn vision into reality."
- **EY** — "Shape the future with confidence." Confidence-as-brand. "Augmenting human potential," "exponential value."

**Tier 2 — Mid-Size:**
- **Thoughtworks** — **Notable outlier.** "AI that works." Conversational, uses humor ("No consultant crowds. And finance can open the bill without emergency lighting.") Specific metrics with personality.
- **Slalom** — "AI, hold the hype." Explicitly anti-hype branding — but still says "drive impact," "human-centered," "scalable" underneath.
- **Avanade** — "Unlock," "Unleash," "No complexity, no delays, just impact." Standard enterprise marketing, indistinguishable from dozens of others.

**Tier 3 — Boutique:**
- **Bosio Digital** — **Closest to Sherpa.** "Humans First." "No pressure, no pitch deck — just an honest conversation." Explicitly attacks Big 4 model.
- **Agathon** — **Radically direct.** "Success means you don't need me anymore." Solo-practitioner confidence. First person. Anti-dependency.
- **Vstorm** — Technical credibility through specificity. "30+ agentic projects." Numbers-forward.
- **LatticeFlow AI** — "AI Governance. Done Right." Evidence-based, governance-focused. Product company, not consulting.

### Universal Buzzwords (6+ of 13 firms)

Transform/transformation, at scale, unlock, end-to-end, drive impact/real results, human-centered, competitive advantage.

### The Anti-Hype Counter-Pattern

Three firms explicitly anti-hype but on a spectrum:
- Slalom: anti-hype as marketing wrapper around conventional language
- Bosio: anti-hype as business model, plain language throughout
- Agathon: anti-hype through radical specificity

### Key Differentiators for Sherpa

1. **Sherpa's avoid-list is validated.** Every avoided word appears prominently across surveyed firms.
2. **"Anti-hype" alone is not differentiation.** Slalom owns it at scale, Bosio in boutique. Becoming its own genre.
3. **"We use what we ship" is the strongest differentiator.** No other firm makes this claim.
4. **"Calm, Not Cold" is genuinely rare.** Nobody writes with calm warmth. Firms are either bombastic, cold, framework-jargon, or aggressively anti-hype. Sherpa's "colleague who explains clearly" is an empty lane.
5. **Specific risk: sounding like Bosio.** Close enough to confuse. Key differences to amplify: Sherpa has a framework, avoids competitive positioning, leads with practice evidence.

## Implications

Sherpa's "warm competence" voice genuinely occupies an empty lane. The strongest move is leading with "we use what we ship" — structural differentiation, not tonal. The avoid-list is validated against real competitor language. Risk is sounding like Bosio Digital; mitigation is avoiding competitive positioning and leading with framework evidence.
