# Vector 6: Bellingham/Whatcom County Local Programs

**Question:** What grants, programs, and small business resources are available specifically in Bellingham and Whatcom County?
**Agent dispatched:** 2026-03-18

## Findings

### Free Advisory & Mentoring (Start Here)

- **WWU Small Business Development Center (SBDC)** — Free, confidential one-on-one advising from Certified Business Advisors. Operating since 1983. Funded by SBA, City of Bellingham, Port of Bellingham, and Whatcom County. Contact: 360-650-7232, sbdc@wwu.edu.
- **SCORE Bellingham** — Free mentoring from retired business professionals. 275+ collective years of experience. 20+ years serving Whatcom and Skagit counties.
- **Choose Whatcom** — One-stop portal co-funded by City of Bellingham, Port of Bellingham, and Whatcom County.
- **NW Innovation Resource Center (NWIRC)** — Free 501(c)(3) incubator/accelerator. Offers Just-In-Time Mentorship, business strategy, AI Skills & Data Analytics training programs. Located at 2200 Rimland Dr, Ste 210, Bellingham. Contact: 360-255-7870, info@nwirc.com.

### Direct Grant Programs

- **Downtown Bellingham Partnership Storefront Improvement Grant** — Up to $2,000 for street-level facade improvements. Must be downtown. 2026 cycle closed Dec 10, 2025.
- **Downtown Bellingham Revitalization Grants** — Broader revitalization funding for downtown businesses.
- **City of Bellingham Fan Zone Grants** — $50,000 in awards up to $5,000 each for storefront improvements.
- **Project Neighborly (City + Whatcom Community Foundation)** — Grants up to $5,000 for community-building projects. 2026 cycle closed Feb 23, awards announced April 2026.
- **Whatcom Community Foundation Nonprofit Fatigue Grant** — $1K-$10K for nonprofits only. Open through May 11, 2026.

### Tax Incentives

- **Downtown Urban Village Tax Credit** — Graduated B&O credit: 90% year 1, 75% year 2, 50% year 3.
- **WA Small Business B&O Tax Credit** — Sliding scale; below threshold = zero B&O tax.
- **Advanced Computing R&D Tax Credit** — Up to $2M annual B&O credit for high-tech R&D. Potentially applicable.
- **International Services B&O Tax Credit** — Credits for new employment in computer, data processing, business consulting.

### State Capital/Lending Programs

- **WA SSBCI Venture Capital Program** — $49M targeting underserved startups including AI/ML.
- **Small Business Flex Fund 2** — $63M SSBCI-funded lending.
- **Revenue Based Financing Fund** — $12M, repayment tied to earnings percentage (not equity).
- **SSBCI Technical Assistance Program** — $2M for disadvantaged/very small business owners.

### Technology Community

- **Technology Alliance Group for NW Washington (TAGNW)** — 501(c)(3) since 1999. Monthly happy hours, Lunch & Learns, community groups (Analytics, Cybersecurity, Digital Marketing, IT). Annual TAG Summit. Contact: 360-312-7105.
- **Bellingham Entrepreneurs** — Startup Academy, Pitch Fest, Founders' Roundtable, annual Startup Challenge, Innovation Week (November).
- **Innovate Bellingham** — Community innovation hub.
- **Invent Coworking** — Downtown coworking. Hosts tech/startup events.

### Investment (Equity — Not Grants)

- **Bellingham Angel Investors (BAI)** — 35 members, $30M+ invested in ~50 companies since 2005. $75K-$400K typical. Technology/healthcare popular.

### Infrastructure

- **Port of Bellingham Innovation Zone** — State-designated IPZ on Waterfront District. 10,000 sq ft Technology Development Center. Access to WWU research and BTC workforce training. Port serves as county's ADO. Contact: econdev@portofbellingham.com, 360-676-2500.

### Other Organizations

- **Sustainable Connections** — Local business network, "Think Local First."
- **Whatcom Business Alliance** — Private sector advocacy. Contact: 360-746-0418.
- **BUILD Whatcom** — New alliance announced January 2026.
- **Bellingham Regional Chamber of Commerce** — Networking, Leadership Whatcom.

## Implications

1. Free advisory (SBDC, SCORE, NWIRC) should be the first engagement — zero cost, professional guidance.
2. Most local grants target retail/physical businesses. A consulting company without a storefront has limited eligibility.
3. TAGNW is the technology community hub. Monthly events are a natural pipeline for clients and collaborators.
4. Port of Bellingham Innovation Zone is worth investigating — Technology Development Center with WWU/BTC access.
5. Tax incentives (B&O credits, Advanced Computing R&D credit) may provide immediate value.
