# Vector 1: WA State Commerce Grants

**Question:** What grant programs does the Washington State Department of Commerce offer for small businesses, technology companies, or consulting firms in 2025-2026?
**Agent dispatched:** 2026-03-17

## Findings

### Programs with direct relevance to a small AI consulting company:

- **The Spark Act (HB 1833) — AI-specific grant program.** Creates a "Spark Act Grant Program" administered by WA Department of Commerce specifically for AI innovation by small businesses. Prioritizes applicants who commit to ethical AI use, complete a risk analysis, and propose projects with statewide impact. The AI Task Force assists Commerce in identifying annual grant priorities. Passed the House 96-1 on Feb 17, 2026, but was returned to House Rules on March 12, 2026 after Senate Ways & Means took no action. **Status: not yet law. No funding appropriated yet.** If it passes, this would be the single most directly relevant program for Sherpa.

- **STEP Export Voucher Program — temporarily halted.** Reimburses up to $10,000/year (three vouchers max) for export-related expenses: trade shows, translation, international marketing, IP protection, travel. Requires 25% cash match. **Halted as of Oct 1, 2025** pending SBA redesign guidance. Contact: Step.Vouchers@Commerce.wa.gov.

- **Federal SBIR/STTR Grants — expired authorization.** The core federal grant program for small tech companies ($150K Phase 1, $1M+ Phase 2). Consulting companies can qualify as prime applicants if developing novel technology (not just providing services). **Congressional authorization expired Sept 30, 2025**, no new solicitations until reauthorized. A 2026 Senate bill aims to rescue the program. WA had a $25,000 SBIR/STTR Matching Fund through Life Science Washington Institute, but it completed its final year in 2025.

### Programs that exist but are poor fits:

- **CERB** — Applicants must be local governments or tribes for public infrastructure. Not applicable.
- **Governor's Economic Development Strategic Reserve Fund** — Highly strategic, large-scale industrial projects. Not designed for consulting/software.
- **Innovation Cluster Accelerator Program (ICAP)** — Awards $50K-$150K for "industry-led consortia." Sherpa could participate in a cluster but cannot apply solo.
- **Evergreen Manufacturing Growth Grants** — Manufacturing only.
- **Small Business Innovation Fund (SBIF)** — $32.5M awarded in 2022 to intermediary organizations (incubators, accelerators), not directly to individual businesses. No current cycle.
- **Green Jobs Grant Program 2025** — Potentially relevant only if Sherpa positions AI work as enabling clean energy outcomes.
- **Community Reinvestment Program (CRP) Business Grants** — $3,500-$10,000 through local WDCs. King County applications open March 2-April 6, 2026. **Prioritizes BIPOC-owned businesses.**
- **WSMA Microenterprise Grants** — Goes to 501(c)(3) nonprofits, not businesses directly.
- **Innovation Partnership Zones** — A designation program, not a direct grant.

### Private/corporate (expired):

- **Microsoft AI for Good Open Call** — $5M for WA-based entities. Deadline was Feb 17, 2025. No 2026 cycle announced yet.

## Implications

The honest assessment is bleak for direct, currently-available WA state grants. No currently open program directly targets small AI/tech consulting companies. The Spark Act (HB 1833) is the single most promising but is not yet law. SBIR/STTR would be the natural federal fit but is currently unauthorized. The most realistic path for an early-stage AI consulting company in WA right now is federal or private, not state.
