# Vector 2: Federal Grant Programs

**Question:** What federal grant programs (beyond SBIR/STTR) are available to small AI/technology consulting companies?
**Agent dispatched:** 2026-03-18

## Findings

### Critical Context: DOGE Disruption

The federal grant landscape is in significant turbulence. DOGE terminated ~16,000 grants totaling ~$49B, including $1B in already-awarded NSF grants. NTIA's entire Digital Equity Act ($2.75B) was terminated May 2025. MBDA's 41 business centers had all grants terminated. However, Congress pushed back in FY2026 appropriations — NSF secured $8.75B. Programs framed around "national security" and "AI infrastructure" survived better than DEI-adjacent programs.

### Tier 1: Directly Relevant and Currently Open

- **SBIR/STTR (Reauthorized Feb 2026)** — Reauthorized through Sept 2031 via the Small Business Innovation and Economic Security Act (S. 3971). Agencies restarting solicitations: DoD/NIH March-April 2026, NSF/DOE/NASA April-May, smaller agencies May-June. New "Strategic Breakthrough Awards" up to $30M/48 months (100% matching). Phase I ~$275K-$300K, Phase II up to $1.75M.

- **NSF PESOSE (Pathways to Enable Secure Open-Source Ecosystems)** — **Single most aligned federal program.** NSF issued a Dear Colleague Letter (Feb 20, 2026) specifically calling for proposals on open protocols for AI agent ecosystems, interoperability standards, and security for open-source AI infrastructure. For-profit LLCs explicitly eligible. Track 1 (Scoping): up to **$300K/1 year** (~30 awards). Track 2 (Establishing): up to **$1.5M/2 years** (~10 awards). Track 3 (Security): up to **$1.5M/2 years** (~10 awards). **Deadline: September 1, 2026.** Must be U.S.-based, >50% U.S.-owned. University partnerships strongly preferred.

- **NSF Convergence Accelerator — Northwest Region** — Expanding from 1 national to 10 regional accelerators. **Northwest region (WA, OR, AK, parts of Northern CA) launches summer 2026.** Phase 1: up to **$750K/9 months**; Phase 2: up to **$5M/2 years**. Focus areas not yet announced.

### Tier 2: Worth Investigating, Partial Fit

- **EDA Tech Hubs Program** — $220M in FY2025 implementation awards for designated Tech Hub consortia. Sherpa could participate as a sub-awardee if Bellingham/Whatcom is adjacent to a designated hub.

- **EDA Build to Scale — Venture Challenge** — Funds intermediary organizations supporting tech startups. Sherpa as a beneficiary, not applicant. Stage II deadline Feb 18, 2026 (closed).

- **DOL Industry-Driven Skills Training Fund** — $30M, grants up to $8M to State Workforce Agencies. AI infrastructure is a listed priority. Sherpa's angle: subcontractor delivering AI workforce training.

- **DOL Strengthening Community Colleges (Round 6)** — $65M, grants of $6.5-$10.8M. **Deadline: May 20, 2026.** Sherpa could partner with Whatcom Community College or Bellingham Technical College as AI training technology partner.

- **NIST Manufacturing Extension Partnership** — $175M in FY2026 + $20M for AI in manufacturing centers. Sherpa could subcontract to deliver AI/digital transformation services.

### Tier 3: Emerging / Not Yet Available

- **Small Business AI Training Act of 2026** — Bipartisan bill (Sen. Cantwell, WA / Moran) authorizing Commerce/SBA grant programs for AI training organizations. At least 25% to rural/underserved communities (Bellingham could qualify). **Status: introduced Feb 2026, not yet law.**

- **NSF CyberAICorps Scholarship for Service** — Innovation Track up to $500K for educational institutions. University partnership path. Target: April 3, 2026.

### Tier 4: Confirmed Dead / Not Applicable

- **NTIA Digital Equity Act** — $2.75B terminated May 2025.
- **MBDA Business Centers** — All 41 grants terminated by DOGE.
- **SBA direct grants** — SBA explicitly does not provide grants for starting/expanding a business.

## Implications

1. **NSF PESOSE is the bullseye.** Sherpa's open-source AI agent collaboration framework with MCP integration, behavioral agent definitions, and governance conventions maps directly to the DCL. September 1, 2026 deadline gives 6 months. University partnership (WWU CS?) would strengthen application.
2. **SBIR is back.** Reauthorization changes the calculus. NSF AI topic restarts April-May 2026. Phase I ~$275K requires no matching funds.
3. **The DOGE factor is real.** Applications should frame work as "national competitiveness," "AI infrastructure," "workforce readiness" — not equity or social outcomes.
4. **Bellingham's rural-adjacent positioning is an asset.** Multiple programs have rural carve-outs.
5. **Workforce training angle is strong but indirect** — Sherpa as subcontractor/partner to community colleges.
