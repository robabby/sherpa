# Iteration 1 — 2026-03-17/18

## Findings

### Vector 1: WA State Commerce Grants
**Question:** What state-level grants exist for small tech/consulting businesses?
**Full report:** [iteration-1/vector-1-wa-state-commerce-grants.md](iteration-1/vector-1-wa-state-commerce-grants.md)

- **No currently open WA state grant directly targets small AI/tech consulting companies.** Programs exist for manufacturing, infrastructure, clean energy, underserved communities, and intermediary nonprofits.
- **The Spark Act (HB 1833)** would create an AI innovation grant through Commerce, but stalled in the Senate March 12, 2026.
- **CRP Business Grants** ($3,500-$10,000) are the only currently-open program with possible eligibility, but prioritize BIPOC-owned businesses.

**Implications:** State grants are not a viable near-term path. Programs serve different sectors.

### Vector 2: Federal Grant Programs
**Question:** What federal grants exist beyond SBIR/STTR?
**Full report:** [iteration-1/vector-2-federal-grants.md](iteration-1/vector-2-federal-grants.md)

- **SBIR/STTR was reauthorized through Sept 2031** (Feb 2026). Phase I ~$275K-$300K. NSF/DOE/NASA solicitations restart April-May 2026. New "Strategic Breakthrough Awards" up to $30M.
- **NSF PESOSE is the single most aligned federal program.** Dear Colleague Letter (Feb 20, 2026) specifically calls for proposals on open protocols for AI agent ecosystems, interoperability, and open-source AI infrastructure. Track 1: up to $300K/1yr. Track 2/3: up to $1.5M/2yr. **Deadline: September 1, 2026.** For-profit LLCs explicitly eligible. University partnerships strongly preferred.
- **NSF Convergence Accelerator — Northwest Region** launches summer 2026. Phase 1: up to $750K/9mo. Phase 2: up to $5M/2yr.
- **DOGE disruption is real** — applications should frame as "national competitiveness" and "AI infrastructure."
- **DOL Strengthening Community Colleges** — $65M, deadline May 20, 2026. Sherpa as technology partner to local community colleges.

**Implications:** NSF PESOSE is a near-perfect match for Sherpa's framework. SBIR reauthorization reopens the main federal path. Bellingham's rural-adjacent position is an asset for multiple programs.

### Vector 4: AI/Tech-Specific Grants and Foundations
**Question:** What private foundations, tech accelerator grants, or AI-specific funding exist?
**Full report:** [iteration-1/vector-4-ai-tech-foundation-grants.md](iteration-1/vector-4-ai-tech-foundation-grants.md)

- **NLnet NGI Zero Commons Fund** — EUR 5K-50K. **Deadline: April 1, 2026.** Calls every 2 months. Strong fit.
- **Sovereign Tech Fund** — EUR 50K+ for open digital base technologies. Rolling. Excludes user-facing apps.
- **Cloud credit programs** — Microsoft Founders Hub ($5K, no VC), Google ($2K-$350K), AWS ($100K-$1M), Anthropic ($1.3K-$150K+).
- **Anthropic Economic Futures** — $10-50K + API credits. Rolling. Human+AI productivity research.
- **GitHub Secure OSS Fund** — $10K + $150K Azure credits. Watch for 2026 cycle.

**Implications:** Open-source ecosystem has mature funding for exactly what Sherpa builds. Framing must emphasize infrastructure, not consulting.

### Vector 5: Grant Readiness Requirements
**Question:** What does Sherpa need before applying?
**Full report:** [iteration-1/vector-5-grant-readiness.md](iteration-1/vector-5-grant-readiness.md)

- **Registration chain:** EIN → SAM.gov (UEI, 7-10 business days) → Grants.gov.
- **SAM.gov requires notarized Entity Administrator Letter** (most common rejection reason). Renews annually.
- **NAICS codes:** Primary 541519 (Computer Related Services) or 541611 (Management Consulting).
- **Common docs:** Business plan, 2-3yr tax returns, P&L, balance sheet, Articles of Organization, LLC Operating Agreement.
- **SBA does not give direct grants** for starting/expanding businesses. Exception: SBIR/STTR.

**Implications:** SAM.gov registration is the critical path — 7-10 day lead time, notarized letter required. Start immediately.

### Vector 6: Bellingham/Whatcom County Local Programs
**Question:** What grants and resources exist specifically in Bellingham and Whatcom County?
**Full report:** [iteration-1/vector-6-bellingham-whatcom-local.md](iteration-1/vector-6-bellingham-whatcom-local.md)

- **Free advisory:** WWU SBDC (free advising, 360-650-7232), SCORE Bellingham (free mentoring), NWIRC (free incubator/accelerator with AI skills training, 2200 Rimland Dr).
- **Technology community:** TAGNW (tech networking, Analytics/Cyber/IT groups), Bellingham Entrepreneurs (Startup Academy, Pitch Fest).
- **Port of Bellingham Innovation Zone** — State-designated IPZ on Waterfront District. 10,000 sq ft Technology Development Center with WWU/BTC access. Port is county's ADO.
- **Local grants are retail-focused.** Downtown storefront improvement, revitalization — not designed for tech consulting.
- **Tax incentives:** B&O Small Business Credit (potentially zero tax), Advanced Computing R&D Credit ($2M cap), Downtown Urban Village graduated credit.
- **State capital programs:** SSBCI Venture Capital ($49M targeting AI/ML), Revenue Based Financing Fund ($12M, earnings-based repayment).

**Implications:** Free advisory (SBDC, SCORE, NWIRC) should be first engagement. Local grants aren't a fit, but the technology community and Innovation Zone are strategic resources.

## Synthesis

Five themes emerge across all vectors:

### 1. The funding landscape has three tiers with very different fit levels

| Tier | Source | Fit | Timeline |
|------|--------|-----|----------|
| **Best fit** | NSF PESOSE, SBIR, NLnet, Sovereign Tech Fund | Framework is exactly what they fund | 3-6 months |
| **Good fit** | Anthropic Economic Futures, cloud credits, GitHub Fund | Low-friction, rolling | Immediate |
| **Poor fit** | WA state grants, local Bellingham grants | Wrong sector/type | n/a |

### 2. NSF PESOSE is the single highest-value opportunity

The Feb 20, 2026 Dear Colleague Letter literally calls for proposals on open protocols for AI agent ecosystems and open-source AI infrastructure. Sherpa's MCP server, behavioral agent framework, and governance conventions are a direct match. Up to $1.5M over 2 years. Deadline September 1, 2026.

### 3. SBIR reauthorization changes everything

The four-month lapse created a false signal. SBIR is back through 2031, NSF AI topics restart April-May 2026, and Phase I provides ~$275K with no matching requirement. This is the main federal R&D funding path.

### 4. Bellingham's value is advisory and community, not grants

The local ecosystem provides free expert advising (SBDC), mentorship (SCORE), incubator access (NWIRC), and tech community (TAGNW) — but not direct grants. These advisory resources are strategically valuable for preparing stronger grant applications.

### 5. Grant readiness requires immediate action

SAM.gov registration (7-10 day lead time + notarized letter) gates all federal opportunities. The NLnet April 1 deadline is 14 days out. The registration chain and business plan preparation should start this week.
