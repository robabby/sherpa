Competitive Landscape: Agentic Consulting Platforms Q1 2026

2026-03-21 · competitive-analysis

Executive Summary

The agentic consulting landscape has shifted dramatically since Q4 2025. Three categories of players have emerged: platform vendors selling horizontal AI infrastructure, consulting-first firms packaging AI expertise as services, and hybrid operators combining framework IP with delivery capacity.

Sherpa occupies a unique position as a hybrid operator with open convention-layer IP — the framework is the differentiator, not the model or the infrastructure.

Market Segments

Platform Vendors

  • Deloitte AI Studio — Enterprise-scale, SOC2-certified, $50k minimum engagement. Targeting Fortune 500 with "AI transformation" packages that bundle consulting + platform access.
  • Accenture myNav — Internal AI platform now being externalized. Strong on compliance verticals (healthcare, financial services). Pricing is opaque but estimated at $200-500/hr for consulting + platform fees.
  • McKinsey Lilli — Knowledge management AI, not agentic orchestration. Relevant as a competitive reference but different category.

Framework-First Players

  • LangChain / LangSmith — Developer-focused, open source framework + commercial observability. No consulting arm but strong community adoption. Risk: commoditizes the orchestration layer that Sherpa builds on.
  • CrewAI — Multi-agent framework with "crews" concept. Growing enterprise interest but no governance or convention system. Complementary rather than competitive.
  • AutoGen (Microsoft) — Research-oriented, strong on multi-agent conversation patterns. Enterprise adoption via Azure integration. Different positioning but overlapping mindshare.

Key insight: No player in the market combines a behavioral agent framework with a consulting delivery model. The closest comparisons are either pure-platform (LangChain) or pure-consulting (Deloitte). Sherpa's convention-layer approach is genuinely novel.

Strategic Implications

Three opportunities emerge from this analysis:

  • Convention portability — Sherpa's defineConfig() and behavioral agent definitions are platform-agnostic. Position the framework as the governance layer that works across any LLM backend, not tied to a single provider.
  • SMB gap — Enterprise players price out small and mid-size organizations. Sherpa's "thumb drive test" (install and demo in 60 seconds) directly targets the underserved SMB segment.
  • Proof through use — WavePoint as a live customer reference demonstrates that the framework works in production. No competitor has an equivalent public case study with this level of transparency.
