# Vector 1: Recursive Discovery Frameworks in Consulting

**Question:** How do top consulting firms (McKinsey, Bain, BCG) and agile coaches structure organizational discovery? What AI-augmented discovery tools exist? How does Sherpa's four-question recursive framework compare?
**Agent dispatched:** 2026-03-16

## Findings

### MBB Diagnostic Frameworks

**Bain: Decisions as the organizing unit (closest to Sherpa Q1)**

- Bain's entire organizational design practice is built on the thesis that "a company's structure results in better performance only if it improves the organization's ability to make and execute key decisions better and faster than competitors." Published in HBR ["The Decision-Driven Organization" (2010)](https://hbr.org/2010/06/the-decision-driven-organization) and their [Decision Effectiveness practice](https://www.bain.com/consulting-services/organization/decision-effectiveness/).
- Three concrete steps: (1) list critical decisions, (2) determine where in the org those decisions should be made, (3) organize structure around sources of value. ([Bain: Decision-Driven Organization](https://www.bain.com/insights/decision-driven-organization/))
- The **"Decision X-ray"** diagnostic: asks everyone involved in a selected decision to rate it on four dimensions — quality, speed, execution yield, and effort. Nike used this to analyze 33 critical decisions. ([Bain: Focus on Key Decisions](https://www.bain.com/insights/decision-insights-2-focus-on-key-decisions/))
- **RAPID** (Recommend, Agree, Perform, Input, Decide) role-assignment tool for each decision. ([Bain: RAPID](https://www.bain.com/insights/rapid-decision-making/))
- **Org Navigator** provides a "detailed view across all relevant parameters." 4,000+ organizational design projects completed. ([Bain: Org Navigator](https://www.bain.com/consulting-services/organization/decision-and-organization-diagnostic/))

**McKinsey: Health as the organizing unit**

- **Organizational Health Index (OHI)**: 43 management practices across 9 dimensions — Direction, Leadership, Work Environment, Accountability, Coordination & Control, Capabilities, Motivation, Innovation & Learning, External Orientation. ([McKinsey OHI](https://www.mckinsey.com/solutions/orgsolutions/overview/organizational-health-index/how-ohi-works))
- 9 dimensions grouped into 3 categories: Alignment, Execution, Renewal. Database of 8M+ survey responses from 2,600+ clients. ([McKinsey OHI overview](https://www.mckinsey.com/solutions/orgsolutions/overview/organizational-health-index))
- **Organize to Value** uses AI to "map operating model choices, identifying an organization's unique fingerprint and benchmarking against a customized set of aspirational peers." ([McKinsey: Organize to Value](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/people-and-organizational-performance/how-we-help-clients/organize-to-value))

**BCG: Capability maturity as the organizing unit**

- **IT Capability Maturity Framework (IT-CMF)**: benchmarks IT functions against industry peers, highlights capability gaps with global benchmarking. ([BCG: Getting Fit for Transformation](https://www.bcg.com/publications/2015/it-capability-maturity-framework-technology-business-transformation-getting-fit-for-transformation))

### Established Organizational Design Frameworks

- **Galbraith Star Model** (1960s, still dominant): 5 policies — Strategy, Structure, Processes, Rewards, People. "Structure determines the location of decision-making power." ([Galbraith Star Model](https://jaygalbraith.com/services/star-model/))
- **Weisbord Six-Box Model**: Purposes, Structure, Relationships, Rewards, Leadership, Helpful Mechanisms. Each box has a formal system (on paper) and an informal system (what people actually do). ([Weisbord Six-Box Model](https://strategicmanagementinsight.com/tools/weisbords-six-box-model/))
- **McKinsey 7-S**: Strategy, Structure, Systems, Shared Values, Skills, Style, Staff. ([McKinsey 7S](https://www.futureventures.ca/insights/the-mckinsey-7s-framework-a-guide-to-effective-organizational-change))

### Agile/Tech Community: Architecture for Flow

- **Susanne Kaiser's "Architecture for Flow"** combines Wardley Mapping + Domain-Driven Design + Team Topologies into a single discovery-to-design methodology. ([Architecture for Flow](https://architectureforflow.com/), [InfoQ talk](https://www.infoq.com/presentations/ddd-wardley-mapping-team-topology/))
- Discovery starts with: current challenges, team cognitive load surveys, communication/coordination effort mapping, and Wardley Maps. Covers 10 dimensions of organizational agility. ([InfoQ article](https://www.infoq.com/articles/adaptive-socio-technical-systems-flow/))
- Wardley Mapping "makes the system visible, evolutionary, and actionable where other frameworks stop." ([Wardley Maps for Agile](https://www.wardleymaps.com/agile))

### Fractal/Recursive Organizational Theory

- **Sociocracy 3.0** has a formal "Fractal Organization" pattern: self-governing circles with defined domains, sub-circles recursively delegate authority. ([S3: Fractal Organization](https://patterns.sociocracy30.org/fractal-organization.html))
- Academic literature: "The main characteristic of a fractal company is the structural similarity between fractals and the whole company in terms of organizational design." ([ResearchGate: Fractal Organisation Theory](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/272312230_Fractal_Organisation_Theory))

### AI-Augmented Discovery Tools

- **9Lenses**: Platform that lets consultants "interview" everyone in an organization remotely — craft questions once, gather insights at scale. AI-powered Discovery Assistant and Assessment Scorecard. Integrates with Salesforce, HubSpot. Closest existing product to "AI pre-mapping client organizations." ([9Lenses](https://9lenses.com/))
- **Harvard/BCG "Jagged Frontier" study** (2023, 758 BCG consultants): GPT-4 users completed tasks 25.1% faster, 40% higher quality. But for tasks outside AI's frontier, AI users were 19 percentage points less likely to get the right answer. ([HBS Working Paper](https://www.hbs.edu/faculty/Pages/item.aspx?num=64700))
- **Centric Consulting**: AI agents "extract functional requirements from legacy code, reverse-engineer complex architectures, and uncover hidden business rules" — completing in days what traditionally takes weeks. ([Centric Consulting](https://centricconsulting.com/technology-solutions/artificial-intelligence-consulting/ai-augmented-development/))

### Discovery as a Standalone Service

- Industry converging on paid discovery as a standalone product. "The focus is not on problem-solving, but on problem definition. Since problem definition is at least as valuable as problem-solving, you have every right to charge for that." ([The Visible Authority](https://www.thevisibleauthority.com/blog/every-consulting-firm-needs-a-discovery-service))
- Typical pricing: 5-10% of general project value, fixed price and scope. Deliverable: guided presentation with one finding per page. ([Consulting Success](https://www.consultingsuccess.com/discovery-session), [Boutique Consulting Club](https://boutiqueconsultingclub.com/blog/paid-discovery))

## Implications

**Where Sherpa's framework aligns with established practice:**

1. **Q1 ("What decisions?") directly validated by Bain.** Their entire org design practice starts from the same premise. Difference: Bain redesigns Fortune 500 org charts at $2M+ engagements. Sherpa generates `defineConfig()` nodes in a 30-minute conversation. Same intellectual foundation, radically different delivery.

2. **Q2 ("What information?") maps to well-established information quality research.** Sherpa makes it operational (data integrations, knowledge base seeding) rather than academic.

3. **Q3 ("What quality?") aligns with McKinsey's OHI Accountability dimension and Galbraith's Rewards policy.** Sherpa's innovation: machine-readable conventions (`.claude/rules/`) rather than aspirational slide decks.

4. **Q4 ("What breaks?") is genuinely novel.** No established framework explicitly starts from "what's currently failing" as a first-class discovery question. The "Trails" concept has no direct analog.

**Where Sherpa genuinely differentiates:**

- **Recursive self-similarity.** None of the MBB frameworks are recursive. OHI is a flat survey. Bain's Decision X-ray examines decisions but doesn't recurse. Sherpa's pattern is closer to Sociocracy 3.0's fractal circles than to any consulting framework.

- **Discovery output IS deployment input.** Every other framework produces a report. Sherpa produces a `defineConfig()` that deploys software. Zero gap between understanding and configuration.

- **AI pre-mapping as standard step.** 9Lenses automates survey collection, but no methodology uses AI to generate a draft organizational map before the consultant arrives. The Harvard/BCG "jagged frontier" study warns AI pre-analysis can be confidently wrong — the human refinement step is essential, not optional.

- **Scale-invariant methodology.** Same four questions work for Shayna (30 min, 1 node) and enterprise CTO (multi-level workshops). MBB frameworks require 100+ survey respondents (OHI) or interviewing "everyone involved" (Decision X-ray).

**Where the framework could be strengthened:**

- Bain's "decision value x frequency" formula for prioritizing critical decisions could sharpen Q1 at scale.
- Wardley Mapping's evolution axis is missing — which capabilities are evolving vs commoditized.
- Weisbord's "informal vs formal" gap is absent — Q3 asks what quality means but not what people actually do vs what the handbook says.

## Open Questions

1. How does AI pre-mapping handle the "jagged frontier"? If the AI pre-map is confidently wrong about a node, will the consultant catch it (anchoring bias risk)?
2. Should Sherpa add Q5 ("What's changing?") and Q6 ("What do people actually do vs say?") — or keep 4 and capture these as sub-questions?
3. How deep should recursion go in practice? Max 3 nesting levels per initiative convention, but enterprise clients with 50+ departments could generate enormous config trees.
4. Should the framework produce a Bain-style prioritized "critical decisions list" as an intermediate artifact before mapping to config?
5. How does this compare to 9Lenses commercially? Potential integration partner, competitor, or irrelevant because Sherpa outputs software config, not reports?
6. Pricing discovery as standalone service: industry standard is 5-10% of project value, but Sm tier ($2-5K total) puts discovery at $200-500. Bundle discovery + deployment for Sm/Md, standalone discovery only for Lg/Enterprise?
