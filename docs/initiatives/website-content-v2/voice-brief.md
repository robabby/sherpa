## Named Enemy

**AI transformation theater** — companies adopting AI without governance, consultants selling "AI strategy" that's really PowerPoint, the gap between what AI can do and how organizations are actually managing it.

But the deeper enemy is **careless displacement** — agentic systems that eliminate jobs without creating new opportunity. The technology is profound, but the current model doesn't account for the people it displaces.

---

## Named Trade-offs (What We Refuse)

- Would rather lose an engagement than win it with inflated promises
- Won't pretend to be the expert when everyone is learning
- Won't build dependency — the framework stays when we leave, the knowledge transfers
- Won't separate "what we sell" from "what we use" — same tools for consulting and open-source

---

## Pronoun Decision

**"We"** — inclusive company voice, but grounded in personal experience. "We" serves three purposes:
1. The Sherpa team (however small)
2. The inclusive "we" — we're all navigating this together
3. The Foundation Stone register — already established and compelling

Personal "I" may appear in the origin story or about page when appropriate, but the primary voice is "we."

---

## Register Decision

**Both personal and processed.** The personal grounds it ("we watched AI do our work and knew the job was over"). The processed gives it weight ("not as prophecy, but as pattern recognition"). The Foundation Stone is the upper register. Conversational conviction is the lower register. Both belong on the homepage.

## Homepage Audience

The homepage speaks to the **shared human condition** of navigating AI transition — not exclusively to technical teams. Anyone who recognizes the shift is real and wants to navigate it with rigor and care.

Deeper pages handle persona-specific content:
- `/framework` → Practitioners (engineers, builders)
- `/consulting` → Technical Leaders and Executives
- `/learn` → Everyone (AI literacy, thought leadership)

## The Competitor-Swap Test

Before any sentence goes on the homepage, ask: "Could Deloitte say this? Could Accenture? Could any AI consultancy?" If yes, cut it.

**Passes the test (only Sherpa would say this):**
- "We're all learning how to use it. We just bring the rigor."
- "Governance is the new sexy."
- "We saw it coming — not as prophecy, but as pattern recognition."
- "Create opportunity just as much as we automate it."

**Fails the test (anyone could say this):**
- "Ship AI workflows you can actually trust." (v1 hero — generic)
- "Honest guidance for teams shipping AI." (v1 consulting — could be anyone)
- "91% of C-suite execs admit faking AI knowledge." (borrowed stat — anyone can cite it)
