# Vector 2: Anti-Template Homepage Copy

**Question:** What are examples of websites praised for breaking from corporate homepage convention? What structural and tonal choices do they make? What do they omit?
**Agent dispatched:** 2026-03-21

## Findings

### Core Insight

Sites praised for breaking convention lead with a point of view, not a value proposition. The conventional hero→features→proof→CTA formula is designed for companies that haven't figured out what they actually believe.

### Specific Site Examples

**37signals / Basecamp / HEY** — Gold standard for opinionated company voice.
- 1999 manifesto was 37 beliefs about web design. No features, no pricing, no CTA. Built one of the most influential companies in tech.
- HEY features founders' personal email addresses on homepage with note they don't have assistants. Anti-corporate positioning through *behavior*, not copywriting.
- Omit: Customer logos, enterprise tier, "trusted by X companies" banner.

**Stripe** — Confidence through specificity and exclusion.
- Early tagline: "Payments for developers." Seven words. "Developers" intentionally excluded lucrative segments to own a smaller audience completely.
- Copy reads like documentation that happens to be beautiful. No exclamation marks, no superlatives.

**SparkToro / Rand Fishkin** — Founder voice as competitive advantage.
- States goal to be "more of a Zebra than a Unicorn." Explicitly states what they don't do: no SEO, no paid advertising.
- No "Book a Demo" CTAs. No enterprise sales language.

**Gumroad / Sahil Lavingia** — Radical vulnerability as positioning.
- Blog post "Reflecting on My Failure to Build a Billion-Dollar Company" got 100,000+ claps and became the brand story.
- "You just gotta take what you know and sell it." Reads like someone talking, not a brand speaking.

**Linear** — Opinionated product as opinionated copy.
- Spent only $35k on marketing total. "The right opinions for fast moving teams."
- Explicitly positions against flexibility: "there is a default way."
- Dark, minimal design that self-selects the audience.

**Doist (Todoist, Twist)** — Calm as competitive positioning.
- Calls own mission "modest." No exclamation marks. No FOMO. Matches product's calm aesthetic to copy's calm tone.

### What Convention Says to Include vs. What These Sites Do

| Convention | What these sites do instead |
|---|---|
| Stats section | Real stories or nothing. Stats perform authority; stories demonstrate it. |
| Customer logo bar | Absent or placed far down, never above fold |
| Hero CTA ("Book a Demo") | Delayed CTA matching the voice |
| Feature grid | Opinions stated as features |
| "Trusted by X companies" | Founder accessibility, published methodology, radical transparency |

### What They Include That Convention Says to Avoid

- Long personal narrative from founder
- Admitting limitations or failures
- Stating what you refuse to do (self-selects audience)
- Opinions that could alienate some visitors
- First person ("I" not "we") for solo/small firms
- Calling your own mission "modest"

### Alternative Page Flows

- **Manifesto flow:** Belief → evidence → invitation (37signals)
- **Narrative flow:** Problem as story → what we learned → how we work now → invitation (SparkToro, Gumroad)
- **Product-as-proof flow:** Show the thing → explain the opinions behind it → invite (Linear, Stripe)
- **Calm authority flow:** One clear statement → expand gently → no urgency (Doist)

### Anti-Patterns (Trying to Be Authentic When It Fails)

- Performative vulnerability — curated "raw" moments for engagement
- Declaring yourself authentic — "like giving yourself the nickname 'Cool Guy'"
- Copying the anti-corporate playbook — writing a manifesto because 37signals did is cargo-culting
- Humor without framework — one funny headline followed by corporate boilerplate is worse than consistent

## Implications

- The v1 stats section (91%, 95%, 9%) is exactly the pattern every successful example avoids. Stats-as-authority is the defining feature of generic consulting copy.
- The five-section flow is a template structure. Let voice discovery determine the flow.
- "I" is probably right for a solo consultancy. "We" from one person is a lie the visitor can feel.
- Opinions ARE the copy. What does Rob believe that Deloitte would never say? That's the homepage.
- Risk: Don't cargo-cult the anti-corporate playbook. The voice has to come from Rob's perspective.

## Open Questions

- What does Rob refuse to do? Exclusions may be the strongest copy material.
- Does the Trails concept belong on the homepage or a separate page?
- Should the homepage be a living document that updates as the practice evolves?
- What's the role of the framework on the homepage — is it a homepage concern or a separate surface?
