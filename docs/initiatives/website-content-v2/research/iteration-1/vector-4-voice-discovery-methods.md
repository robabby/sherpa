# Vector 4: Voice Discovery Methods

**Question:** How do brand strategists and copywriters actually extract an authentic voice from a founder?
**Agent dispatched:** 2026-03-21

## Findings

### Why Guidelines Alone Don't Work

- Adjective pairs (calm/not cold) are necessary but insufficient — ten writers produce ten interpretations of "calm"
- Guidelines describe a zone, not a voice. They prevent bad copy but don't generate distinctive copy
- LLMs make this worse — trained on millions of bland corporate examples, they default to statistical average of all marketing copy without specific voice material
- **The diagnostic test:** "If your template could apply to a competitor without changing a word, it is not specific enough"

### What Actually Works

**1. Record the founder speaking, then analyze the transcript**
- Copyhackers methodology: (1) gather 3-5 pieces of original founder content, (2) measure sentence length/punctuation/grade level, (3) analyze style — what they argue for/against, signature phrases, (4) compile "voice cheat sheet"
- Written copy is always more edited than speech, but speech reveals natural cadence and vocabulary

**2. Justin Blackman's "Brand Ventriloquism" framework**
- Three measurable components: Vocabulary (word complexity), Tone (emotional quality), Cadence (rhythm via punctuation and sentence length)
- Can be plotted on a grid and compared to competitors — closest to a "voice fingerprint"

**3. The GitPrime example (gold standard case study)**
- Co-founder said "Engineers love building cool shit" in a deep interview. That single authentic phrase became the foundation for ALL positioning
- Key: Came from layered interview technique ("Take me back to what brought you to even think about building it"), probing deeper repeatedly until genuine conviction surfaced

**4. Story mining for "moments, not milestones"**
- Most useful stories are specific scenes: "the rejection email, the dinner-table argument, the 'we might have just found something' realization"
- Replace "we faced challenges" with specific moments

### Specific Exercises

**Exercise 1: The Conviction Excavation (20-30 min)**
Three questions, answered verbally (recorded, not written):
1. "What do you believe about AI governance that your competitors don't?"
2. "What makes you angry about the AI consulting industry?"
3. "What would you change about how organizations adopt AI if you had complete power?"

**Exercise 2: The Layered Origin Interview (30 min)**
Start broad, keep asking "why" and "tell me more about that moment":
- "Take me back to what brought you to even think about building this."
- "What was happening in your life when you decided to start Sherpa?"
- "Tell me about a specific moment when you realized the way AI governance was being done was wrong."
- "What's a time you walked away from something because it violated your principles?"
- "What's the thing you've seen in your work that nobody talks about?"

**Exercise 3: The "Say It Your Way" Rewrite (15 min)**
Take 5 generic AI consulting sentences and rewrite them in Rob's voice:
1. "We help organizations implement AI responsibly."
2. "Our framework ensures governance is built in from the start."
3. "AI adoption fails when governance is an afterthought."
4. "We believe in empowering teams, not creating dependency."
5. "Our approach is grounded in real-world experience."
The gap between generic and rewrite IS the voice.

**Exercise 4: The Read-Aloud Test (10 min)**
Read the Foundation Stone and current homepage copy aloud. Note:
- Where does reading feel natural vs. forced?
- Which phrases make you cringe?
- Which lines sound like you talking to a friend?

**Exercise 5: The "Brand as a Person" Introduction (5 min)**
"If Sherpa walked into a room of CTOs, how would it introduce itself? What would it never say?"

### Testing Voice Distinctiveness

1. **The Swap Test:** Remove brand name. Could a competitor use the copy unchanged? If yes, not distinctive.
2. **The Blind Review:** Mix your content with competitor content, branding removed. If identification accuracy < 60%, voice lacks distinctiveness.
3. **The "Demand Receipts" Test:** When a trait feels generic ("we're honest"), demand a specific sentence that demonstrates it. "What does 'honest' look like in a sentence only Sherpa would write?"

### Common Pitfalls

- Starting with adjectives instead of stories (adjective-first produces zones, not voices)
- Confusing what you believe with what you've lived ("We believe in honesty" is generic; "We would rather lose an engagement than win it with inflated promises" is specific)
- Founder monologues without follow-up probing (the gold is 3-4 questions deep)
- Writing voice guidelines instead of collecting voice artifacts (20 sentences > a tone document)
- Over-scoping the session — one workshop = one outcome (voice only, not positioning or naming)

## Implications

1. **The Foundation Stone already contains genuine voice artifacts.** Lines like "Speed is cheap" and "on an ordinary Tuesday" are specific, opinionated, and would fail the competitor-swap test (in a good way). Mine for more of these, don't start from scratch.

2. **The voice discovery session should be structured as an interview, not a brainstorm.** Use layered questioning. Goal is to get Rob talking naturally about frustrations, convictions, and specific experiences — then extract language patterns.

3. **The trails concept is the strongest voice material available.** Real engagement patterns from real consulting work are, by definition, things no competitor can claim.

4. **The current voice-and-tone doc needs sharpening, not replacement.** What's missing is the layer below: specific sentence examples, DO/DON'T pairs from Rob's actual speech, and the "demand receipts" test.

5. **The voice will be found in the gap between the Foundation Stone and the current homepage.** The Foundation Stone has conviction and specificity. The homepage has neither. Identify what got lost in translation.

## Open Questions

- How much of Rob's natural speech has been captured? Podcast episodes, recorded calls, Slack messages?
- What are Rob's specific "hot takes" about AI governance?
- What does Rob sound like when he's frustrated? Complaints, cleaned up but not sanitized, often produce the most distinctive copy.
- What's the relationship between Rob's voice and Sherpa's voice in a founder-led brand?
