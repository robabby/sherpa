# Agentic Consulting Landscape

## Summary

Research initiative mapping the emerging market of solo operators and micro-teams running AI agent-powered consulting businesses. Findings inform Sherpa Consulting's service design, pricing, content strategy, and framework positioning for Phases 3-4 of the roadmap (sherpa.solar website → blog content workforce → YouTube pipeline → AI literacy program).

## State Snapshot

The agentic consulting market in early 2026 is nascent but validated:

- **Unit economics proven:** Solo+agents achieves $25K-$40K/month at 70-85% margins with 5-8 clients. Tool costs are $100-500/month. AI literacy training commands $895-$75,000/engagement at 85-95% margins.
- **No governance tooling exists:** Every practitioner uses ad-hoc markdown + manual review. Nobody has structured lifecycle management, behavioral agent constraints, or quality gates. This is Sherpa's gap.
- **Quality is the existential risk:** AI code has 1.7x more defects. Deloitte shipped hallucinated government reports twice. The overnight workforce works but "can't tell if what it built is good enough."
- **Outcome-based pricing is winning:** 73% of clients prefer it. Seat-based pricing dropped from 21% to 15% in 12 months. The market is moving from "hours" to "deliverables."
- **Trust dynamics are complex:** Disclosure reduces trust (Schilke & Reimann 2025), but being caught hiding AI is worse. Proactive framing around quality guarantees is the winning strategy.
- **Legal risk is escalating:** Insurers adding AI exclusions to E&O policies. 73 new state AI laws in 2025. Regulated industries require disclosure.

## Rationale

Sherpa's roadmap has four business initiatives (website, blog engine, YouTube pipeline, AI literacy) blocked on understanding the competitive landscape. This research provides:

1. **Pricing guidance** — Outcome-based, three-tier productized menus, $5K-$10K/month retainers
2. **Anti-patterns** — Don't sell one-off builds to <$2K clients, don't promise fully autonomous workflows, don't compete on hourly rate
3. **Positioning** — The governance gap is unoccupied. "Framework-powered consulting" is a novel positioning
4. **Risk register** — Insurance exclusions, regulatory exposure, compound failure in multi-step workflows, pricing pressure from rate commoditization

The research validates that the window is open: the unit economics work, demand exists (91% of C-suite faking AI knowledge), and nobody has governance tooling. But it also surfaces serious risks that must be addressed before launching services.
